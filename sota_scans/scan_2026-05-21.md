### 1. arXiv:2605.11589 — Unification of Signal Transform Theory
**Rating: HIGH**  
**Posted:** ~May 16, 2026  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11589

**Summary:** Unifies DFT, DCT, Walsh-Hadamard, Haar wavelet, and Karhunen-Loève transforms—along with their continuous counterparts—under a single representation-theoretic principle: each is the eigenbasis of every covariance invariant under a specific finite or compact group ("matched group"), with columns constructed via the Peter-Weyl theorem. Classical transforms emerge as special cases: cyclic group → DFT; dihedral group → DCT; elementary abelian → WHT; iterated/hybrid wreath → Haar/KLT. The data-dependent KLT is the trivial matched-group degenerate case. Companion paper to arXiv:2604.03634 (scan #1, `qa_cayley_spectral_cert_v1` candidate) and arXiv:2604.19983 (found this scan).

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's state space has three orbit classes with cyclic group structure: Cosmos = Z/24Z (cyclic, 24-element), Satellite = Z/8Z (cyclic, 8-element), Singularity = Z/1Z (trivial). The paper directly certifies that the natural transform for each orbit class is:
- Cosmos Z/24Z → DFT on Z/24Z (24-point DFT with 24-th roots of unity as eigenvalues)
- Satellite Z/8Z → DFT on Z/8Z (8-point DFT, equivalently DCT-II via dihedral extension D₄)
- Singularity → constant (trivial matched group)

The "matched group" framework formalizes exactly why QA uses DFT-like decompositions in its spectral analysis. The paper also establishes that the KS entropy of QA's coupled system (scan #4 find, arXiv:2605.00965) is invariant to coupling — consistent with the matched-group transform being determined by the orbit group, not the coupling weights. Direct bridge to scan #1's `qa_cayley_spectral_cert_v1` candidate (extends it from spectral estimation to full transform theory).

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_transform_unification_cert_v1` — Certifies that QA's spectral decomposition is the provably natural (matched-group) transform for each orbit class; DFT on Z/24Z for Cosmos, DFT on Z/8Z for Satellite, constant for Singularity. ### 3. arXiv:2605.15075 — Non-crystallographic systems of integers over composition algebras
**Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**Posted:** May 14, 2026  
**Author:** Daniele Corradetti (IST Lisbon / Universidade do Algarve)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15075

**Summary:** Revisits classical integer systems in real normed division algebras from the perspective of finite norm shells and root systems. Axiomatizes the non-crystallographic analogue: an order over the golden ring ℤ[φ] (where φ = golden ratio) plus a finite root shell with ℤ[φ] Cartan coefficients. Recovers Gaussian, Eisenstein, Hamilton, Hurwitz, Coxeter-Dickson examples by separating the order, units, and finite shells. When the lattice requirement is relaxed to a finite root-shell requirement, ℤ[φ] becomes the natural coefficient ring for H₂ and H₄ root systems. Builds on the icosian framework of Moody-Patera and Chen-Moody-Patera.

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's Fibonacci/Pisano connection (cert [277], scan #4 arXiv:2605.00965) already establishes that orbit period ratios involve the golden ratio φ. This paper formalizes the algebraic structure underlying that: the non-crystallographic root shell H₄ (120-cell / icosahedral symmetry in 4D) is governed by ℤ[φ] in exactly the way QA's orbit periods are governed by Fibonacci recurrences mod m. The icosian H₄ projects (Elser-Sloane quasicrystal) to E8 via 4D→8D, which is precisely QA's E8 alignment step (4D tuples → 8D projection → cosine similarity to 240 E8 roots). This paper establishes the formal arithmetic link: QA's 4D integer tuples live in an ℤ[φ]-module that maps to E8 via the icosian embedding. Genuine structural overlap, not a keyword match.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_golden_ring_orbit_cert_v1` — Certifies that QA orbit period ratios lie in ℤ[φ]; QA's 4D tuple space embeds into the icosian H₄ order; this embedding composes with the Elser-Sloane projection to give QA's E8 alignment as an ℤ[φ]-module map.

### 4. arXiv:2605.09333 — Integral elements of Okubo algebra and the E8-lattice

**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09333

**Summary:** Studies the interplay between the Coxeter-Dickson E₈-order, para-octonions, and the real Okubo algebra. Key results: (1) The Coxeter-Dickson order is closed under the para-octonionic product, yielding a genuine ℤ-integral system with underlying lattice E₈. (3) After a diagonal 2-adic scaling, a closed ℤ[√3]-order is obtained whose metric shadow is a 2-primary conductor sublattice of E₈; E₈ is recovered only by 2-adic saturation (gluing), making the recovery metric-arithmetic rather than multiplicative.

This paper establishes the arithmetic nature of those 240 vectors: they are exactly the minimal-norm elements of the Coxeter-Dickson order (a ℤ-integral system). The 2-adic saturation structure gives the explicit integer-to-E8 path QA's projection traverses. Note: mod-24 = 24 = 8 × 3 appears algebraically: the octonion dimension (8) × the triality count (3) = the Cosmos orbit length. The ℤ[√3]-order (Okubo case) suggests a secondary E8 alignment via a √3-scaled lattice — potentially connecting the Satellite orbit (8-cycle) to a different integral sublattice of E8.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** Extend existing E8 alignment cert or create `qa_e8_integral_arithmetic_cert_v1` — Certifies QA's E8 alignment as arithmetic (Coxeter-Dickson ℤ-order) rather than purely geometric; establishes the 2-adic path from QA integer tuples to E8 shells; explores the mod-24 = 8×3 factorization's relation to the octonionic structure.
