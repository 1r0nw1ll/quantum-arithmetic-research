## Key Theoretical Contributions

### 1. Three-Layer QA Pythagorean Taxonomy

**Layer 0: QA Five Families** (Existing)
- Fibonacci, Lucas, Phibonacci, Tribonacci, Ninbonacci
- Defined by mod-24 resonance cycles
- 24-tuple periodicity

**Layer 1: Primitive/Non-Primitive Classification** (NEW)
```
Primitive ⇔ gcd(C, F, G) = 1
where:
  C = 2ed (base leg)
  F = ba (altitude leg)
  G = d² + e² (hypotenuse)
```

**Layer 2: Classical Primitive Subfamilies** (NEW)

**Fermat Family**: `|C - F| = |I| = 1`
- Minimal difference between base and altitude
- Links to existing QA invariant I
- Characteristic: "balanced" triangles

**Pythagoras Family**: `(d - e)² = 1`
- Hypotenuse and base differ by 1: `|G - C| = 1`
- **Critical QA insight**: Occurs when tuple is **1 step off the diagonal** in mod-9/mod-24 lattice
- Characteristic: "near-isosceles" triangles

**Plato Family**: `|G - F| = 2`
- Hypotenuse and altitude differ by 2
- Characteristic: "altitude-heavy" triangles

### 2. Gender Classification (BREAKTHROUGH)

**Male Tuples**:
- All primitive tuples are male
- Non-primitive with gcd ≠ 2

**Female Tuples**:
- gcd(C,F,G) = 2
- Both b and a are even
- Generated by **doubling** any male triangle

**Transformation Rule**:
```
Male (b,e,d,a) → Female (b'=2e, e'=b, d'=a, a'=2d)
```

**Harmonic Interpretation**:
- Female = **octave** of primitive male (2× harmonic)
- Male gcd=3,5,7,... = higher overtones
- Creates literal **harmonic spectrum** of Pythagorean triples

### 3. Harmonicity Index 2.0

```
HI_2.0 = w_ang × H_ang + w_rad × H_rad + w_fam × H_fam
```

**Components**:

**H_ang (Angular Harmonicity)**:

  h_24 = (1 + cos(2πk/24))/2  (mod-24 phase)

## QA Five Families × Primitive Families Cross-Analysis

### Fibonacci → Pythagoras Dominance

**Characteristic**: Dense cluster of Pythagoras primitives `(d-e)² = 1`

**Reason**: Fibonacci recurrence forces d ≈ e except at resonance points

**Example**: (1,2,3,5) → Triangle (5,12,13)
- C=12, F=5, G=13
- gcd=1 (primitive male)
- (d-e)² = (3-2)² = 1 ✓ Pythagoras
- HI_2.0 components: high angular, max radial (1), family contribution (1/3)

**Geometry**: "Almost-isosceles" triangles

### Lucas → Plato Dominance

**Characteristic**: Altitude-skewed triangles with `|G-F| = 2`

**Reason**: Lucas growth rate faster, (b,e,d,a) grows non-symmetrically
- F = ba large relative to C = 2ed

**Geometry**: "Altitude-heavy" triangles

### Phibonacci → Fermat Dominance

**Characteristic**: Highest frequency of Fermat primitives `|C-F| = 1`

**Reason**: Phibonacci tuples oscillate such that ba ≈ 2ed within 1 unit
- "Golden mean balancing" of products vs. sums

**Geometry**: "Balanced" triangles

### Tribonacci/Ninbonacci → Composite Male Dominance

**Characteristic**:
- Large spreads between d and e
- Many non-primitive triangles
- Sparse female (gcd=2) nodes
- Higher gcd values (9, 15, etc.)

**Role**: Feed deep branches of QA Pythagorean tree with elongated triangles
