\begin{abstract}
We introduce the \emph{Path Diversity Index} ($\PDI$), a structural metric
quantifying counterfactual route richness in goal-directed systems.
$\PDI = |M|/R$ counts the fraction of reachable states with $\geq 2$
distinct directed paths from the initial state set, where paths are counted
on the SCC-condensation DAG of the system's generator graph.
$\PDI$ is orthogonal to the Plasticity Index ($\PI$): together they define
four behavioral regimes --- \emph{Frozen}, \emph{Linear Explorer},
\emph{Stuck-Loop Thrashing}, and \emph{Flexible Planner} ---
separated by the \emph{Agency Decomposition Theorem}: $\EA = \AI \times \PDI$.
We prove four structural results: the SCC Saturation Lemma,
No Artificial Inflation Theorem, bounds on $\PDI$ under fan-out and merging
topology, and the Cyclic Thrashing Theorem.
We then connect $\PDI$ to the QA failure algebra via the
\emph{PDI--Obstruction Bridge}: generator failure tags
($\OK$, $\PB$, $\IV$, $\OD$) determine whether obstructions are
topology-preserving (Type~III) or route-reducing (Type~II),
with $\join(\tau(g_1),\tau(g_2)) = \OK$ as the necessary condition
for $\PDI > 0.5$.
Empirically, nine certified reference systems (AI agents, biological,
hybrid) all fall below the $\PDI = 0.5$ threshold (range $0.425$--$0.475$)
in the Linear Explorer regime.
A synthetic FLEXIBLE PLANNER ($\PDI = 0.800$, $\PI = 0.750$) is constructed
with all-$\OK$-tagged cross-link generators, providing the first
constructive existence proof.
A controlled perturbation experiment introduces PARITY\_BLOCK tags
one at a time; $\PDI$ drops by exactly $0.200$ per tag and the regime
collapses from FLEXIBLE PLANNER to LINEAR EXPLORER at $k=2$ tags.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

What makes an agent \emph{flexibly} goal-directed rather than merely
\emph{competent}?
Existing frameworks for system competency --- including Levin's
control-theoretic account of biological cognition
\cite{levin2019regenerative,levin2022technological} and
Fields \& Levin's competency metric suite \cite{fields2022competency} ---
characterize the \emph{size} of a system's reachable state space (Agency
Index, $\AI = R/T$) and the \emph{speed} at which it adapts to perturbation
(Plasticity Index, $\PI = \delta_R/\delta_P$).
Neither metric captures a third structural property:
whether the system has multiple independent routes to its goals ---
the property that distinguishes a system that can
\emph{recover from generator failure} from one that cannot.

We introduce the \emph{Path Diversity Index} ($\PDI$) to fill this gap.
$\PDI$ measures the fraction of reachable states accessible via at least
two distinct directed paths in the SCC-condensation DAG of the generator graph.
Informally: $\PDI$ asks whether the system's reachability structure is a
tree (single route per state) or a DAG with merging branches (multiple
independent routes).

The main contributions of this paper are:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Definition and algorithm} (\S\ref{sec:definition}):
    $\PDI$ via Tarjan SCC-condensation \cite{tarjan1972depth,cormen2022introduction},
    with complexity $O(|V|+|E|)$.
  \item \textbf{Structural theorems} (\S\ref{sec:theory}):
    SCC Saturation Lemma, No Artificial Inflation,
    upper/lower bounds, Cyclic Thrashing Theorem.
  \item \textbf{Regime classification} (\S\ref{sec:regimes}):
    four-quadrant $({\PI}, {\PDI})$ map with the
    Agency Decomposition Theorem $\EA = \AI \times \PDI$.
  \item \textbf{Obstruction algebra bridge} (\S\ref{sec:bridge}):
    failure tags $\to$ obstruction types $\to$ $\PDI$ sensitivity.
  \item \textbf{Experiments} (\S\ref{sec:experiments}):
    nine naturalistic reference systems; synthetic
    existence proof; controlled fragility experiment.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background and Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Generator graph.}
A goal-directed system is modelled as a directed graph $G = (V, E, I, F)$
where $V$ is the state space, $E$ contains a directed edge $(u,v,g)$ whenever
generator $g \in \gens$ maps state $u$ to state $v$,
$I \subseteq V$ is the set of initial states, and $F \subseteq V$ is the set
of goal (attractor) states.
We write $R \subseteq V$ for the set of states reachable from $I$ under any
generator sequence, and $T = |V|$ for the total state count.

\paragraph{SCC-condensation DAG.}
The SCC-condensation $\hat{G}$ of $G$ is computed via Tarjan's algorithm
\cite{tarjan1972depth}: each strongly connected component (SCC) is collapsed
to a single node, and all inter-SCC edges are retained.
$\hat{G}$ is a DAG.
A \emph{trivial SCC} contains a single state with no self-loop;
a \emph{non-trivial SCC} contains a directed cycle.

\paragraph{Existing metrics.}
The QA Competency Detection Framework \cite{QAFamily26Cert} defines:
\[
  \AI = R/T, \quad
  \PI = \delta_R/\delta_P, \quad
  \GD = |F|/T, \quad
  \CE = -\textstyle\sum_g p(g)\ln p(g).
\]
$\AI$ measures control-region extent; $\PI$ measures temporal plasticity
(sensitivity of $R$ to perturbation); $\GD$ measures goal concentration;
$\CE$ measures generator diversity \cite{shannon1948mathematical}.

\section{Path Diversity Index}
\label{sec:definition}

\begin{definition}[Multi-path state]
  State $v \in R$ is a \emph{multi-path state} if there exist at least two
  directed paths $\pi_1 \neq \pi_2$ from some $s \in I$ to $v$ in
  the condensation DAG $\hat{G}$.
  Let $M \subseteq R$ denote the set of all multi-path states.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Path Diversity Index]
  \[
    \PDI(G) \;=\; \frac{|M|}{R}.
  \]
  $\PDI = 0$ iff $G$ is tree-like from $I$ (no state has two independent
  routes); $\PDI = 1$ iff every reachable state is multi-path.
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Computation.}
Run Tarjan's SCC algorithm to obtain $\hat{G}$ in $O(|V|+|E|)$.
Process nodes of $\hat{G}$ in reverse topological order,
maintaining an integer array $\mathrm{cnt}[v]$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item For every non-trivial SCC node $C$:
    if $\mathrm{cnt}[C] \geq 2$, saturate all states in $C$ (set
    $\mathrm{cnt}[v] \leftarrow 2$ for all $v \in C$).
  \item For every trivial SCC node $C = \{v\}$:
    $\mathrm{cnt}[v] \leftarrow \min\bigl(\sum_{u \to v} \mathrm{cnt}[u],\, 2\bigr)$.
\end{itemize}
$|M| = |\{v \in R : \mathrm{cnt}[v] \geq 2\}|$.
Total complexity: $O(|V|+|E|)$.

\section{Structural Theorems}
\label{sec:theory}

\begin{lemma}[SCC Saturation]
  \label{lem:scc_sat}
  Let $C$ be a non-trivial SCC.
  If any state in $C$ is reachable via $\geq 2$ distinct directed paths
  from $I$, then every state in $C$ is reachable via $\geq 2$ distinct
  directed paths.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  By the SCC property, every pair of states in $C$ is mutually reachable
  by an intra-SCC path.
  Let $v \in C$ admit paths $\pi_1, \pi_2$ from $I$.
  For any $w \in C$, let $\rho$ be an intra-SCC path $v \to w$.
  Then $\pi_1 \cdot \rho$ and $\pi_2 \cdot \rho$ are two distinct paths
  from $I$ to $w$ (they agree only if $\pi_1 = \pi_2$, contradiction).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[No Artificial Inflation]
  \label{thm:no_inflate}
  Removing all back-edges from $G$ (i.e., replacing each non-trivial SCC
  by its condensation node) does not decrease $|M|$.
  Formally: naive cycle-aware path-counting on $G$ can only
  \emph{overcount} $|M|$; the SCC-condensation count is a lower bound on
  the true multi-path count.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  Let $\hat{G}$ be the condensation.
  Any path in $G$ that traverses a back-edge within SCC $C$ is not
  edge-disjoint from another path that traverses the same SCC via a
  different back-edge --- they share the SCC entry node.
  Collapsing $C$ to a single node retains the inter-SCC merge count and
  removes spurious intra-SCC ``extra paths.''
  Hence $|M|$ computed on $\hat{G}$ is the canonical count.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Upper Bound under Fan-Out]
  \label{thm:upper}
  If every state in $R$ has at most $k \geq 2$ incoming edges, then
  $\PDI \leq 1 - k/R$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Lower Bound under Regular Merging]
  \label{thm:lower}
  If the condensation DAG is $k$-regular (every non-initial node has
  exactly $k$ predecessors) and $k \geq 2$, then
  $\PDI \geq 1/(k+2)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclic Thrashing]
  \label{thm:thrash}
  If $\PI = 0$ and $\PDI > 0$, then the system contains a non-trivial SCC
  reachable from $I$, and any trajectory initialized in that SCC recurs
  infinitely without reaching any state outside the SCC.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  $\PDI > 0$ implies $|M| > 0$.
  By Lemma~\ref{lem:scc_sat}, $M$ is non-empty only if at least one
  non-trivial SCC is entered via $\geq 2$ routes, or the condensation DAG
  has a merge node.
  $\PI = 0$ implies $\delta_R = 0$: no perturbation expands $R$.
  Hence the system cannot escape its current reachability class.
  A trajectory in a non-trivial SCC cycles indefinitely
  (the SCC has no exit under $\PI = 0$ by definition of zero plasticity).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
  Theorem~\ref{thm:thrash} formalises the \emph{Stuck-Loop Thrashing}
  regime: high path diversity combined with zero plasticity produces
  persistent cycling, not goal attainment.
  Observed empirically in live system traces as Phase~3 PDI~$= 0.889$,
  PI~$= 0$.
\end{remark}

\section{Regime Classification}
\label{sec:regimes}

\begin{theorem}[Agency Decomposition]
  \label{thm:decomp}
  $\AI$ and $\PDI$ are structurally orthogonal:
  $\AI$ depends on BFS reachability diameter, while $\PDI$ depends on
  DAG merge topology.
  The Effective Agency
  \[
    \EA \;=\; \AI \times \PDI
  \]
  decomposes into independent factors: control-region extent and
  counterfactual route richness.
\end{theorem}

The thresholds $\PI = 0.5$ and $\PDI = 0.5$ partition system space into
four regimes (Figure~\ref{fig:regimes}):

\section{PDI--Obstruction Bridge}
\label{sec:bridge}

\paragraph{Failure algebra.}
The QA Failure Algebra \cite{QAFamily76Cert,QAFamily86Cert} defines a
bounded join-semilattice over failure modes
$\carrier = \{\OK, \PB, \IV, \OD\}$ with
$\OK \leq \PB, \IV \leq \OD$ (bottom to top).
Each generator $g$ receives a failure tag $\tau(g) \in \carrier$.

\paragraph{Obstruction types.}
Let $O \subseteq E$ be a named obstruction and $G_O = (V, E \setminus O)$
the residual graph. Write $R_O = R(G_O)$, $M_O = M(G_O)$.

\begin{definition}[Obstruction types]
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Type I} (state-annihilating): $R_O \subsetneq R$.
    \item \textbf{Type II} (route-reducing): $R_O = R$, $M_O \subsetneq M$.
      $\PDI$ strictly decreases.
    \item \textbf{Type III} (topology-preserving): $R_O = R$, $M_O = M$.
      $\PDI$ unchanged.
  \end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Failure-Tag Obstruction Typing]
  \label{thm:bridge}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\tau(g) = \OK$ $\Rightarrow$ obstruction $O_g = \emptyset$
      (Type III vacuously).
    \item $\tau(g) = \PB$ or $\IV$ $\Rightarrow$ $O_g$ is Type I or II
      depending on whether blocked edges are the sole paths to any reachable
      state (Type I) or merely redundant merge edges (Type II).
    \item $\tau(g) = \OD$ $\Rightarrow$ $O_g$ is Type I (permanent
      edge removal).
  \end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Merge Stability Criterion]
  \label{cor:merge}
  $\PDI > 0.5$ is achievable iff there exist $\geq 2$ edge-disjoint directed
  paths to every state in $M$, provided by generators $g_1, g_2$ with
  $\tau(g_1) \join \tau(g_2) = \OK$.
  Any merge-path generator pair with
  $\tau(g_1) \join \tau(g_2) \geq \PB$
  risks collapsing to a single route under a shared blocking condition.
\end{corollary}

\paragraph{Reference set obstruction analysis.}
Of the 16 named obstructions across the nine reference systems,
8 are Type~I (state-annihilating: \texttt{context\_overflow},
\texttt{consensus\_deadlock}, \texttt{energy\_exhaustion}, etc.)
and 9 are Type~II (route-reducing: \texttt{message\_loss},
\texttt{electrode\_drift}, \texttt{tip\_clog}, etc.).
Zero Type~III obstructions appear: no certified exemplar has a
``free'' obstruction that leaves path diversity intact.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Naturalistic Baseline}
\label{sec:baseline}

Nine reference systems were certified under the
QA Competency Detection Framework \cite{QAFamily26Cert}
spanning three substrate domains.
Key findings:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\PDI$ range: $0.425$--$0.475$ (all below the $0.5$ threshold).
  \item Regime distribution: 7/9 \emph{Linear Explorer}
        ($\PI > 0.5$, $\PDI < 0.5$);
        2/9 \emph{Frozen}
        (tool-agent debugger $\PI=0.30$; lab robot $\PI=0.35$).
  \item $\PI$/$\PDI$ orthogonality: bio systems have higher mean
        $\PI$ ($0.71$) than AI agents ($0.56$), yet $\PDI$ is
        statistically indistinguishable across domains
        (range $< 0.02$, Pearson $r < 0.15$).
\end{itemize}

The compressed $\PDI$ band is structurally expected.
Reference systems are curated exemplars with branching-factor-bounded
generator graphs and no deliberate merge engineering.
Achieving $\PDI > 0.5$ requires explicit fan-then-merge topology
absent from current engineering practice.

\subsection{Synthetic Existence Proof}
\label{sec:existence}

\paragraph{Construction.}
A 4-arm, depth-6 grid with cyclic cross-link generators:
\begin{align*}
  \texttt{arm\_advance\_k}&: \texttt{arm}_{k,d} \to \texttt{arm}_{k,d+1},
  \quad \tau = \OK, \\
  \texttt{cross\_link\_k}&: \texttt{arm}_{k,d} \to \texttt{arm}_{(k+1)\%4,d+1},
  \quad \tau = \OK.
\end{align*}
States: apex $s_0$ (depth~0) + $4 \times 6 = 24$ arm states.
Total $R = 25$.

\paragraph{Path-count analysis.}
Depth-$\leq 1$ states have a single path from $s_0$;
depth-$\geq 2$ states each have two distinct paths:
(i)~the within-arm path via \texttt{arm\_advance} and
(ii)~the cross-arm path via \texttt{cross\_link} of the preceding arm.
Hence $|M| = 4 \times 5 = 20$ and
\[
  \PDI = 20/25 = 0.800, \quad \PI = 0.750, \quad \EA = 0.500.
\]
All generators $\tau = \OK$; by Corollary~\ref{cor:merge},
$\join(\OK, \OK) = \OK$ for all merge pairs,
so all obstructions are vacuously Type~III.
\textbf{Regime: \emph{Flexible Planner}.}
This is the first constructive existence proof that the upper-right
quadrant of Figure~\ref{fig:regimes} is non-empty.

\subsection{Controlled Obstruction Perturbation}
\label{sec:perturbation}

\paragraph{Protocol.}
Starting from the synthetic FLEXIBLE PLANNER, we introduce $k$
\textsc{pb} tags on generators $\{\texttt{cross\_link}_i\}_{i<k}$
and recompute $\PDI$ exactly.

\paragraph{Analytical result.}
Blocking $\texttt{cross\_link}_j$ removes the unique merge edge for
$\texttt{arm}_{(j+1)\%4}$; all five depth-$\geq 2$ states of that arm
exit $M$ (Route-Reducing, Type~II obstruction) while remaining in $R$.
Effects are independent across arms.
Therefore:
\[
  \PDI(k) = \frac{20 - 5k}{25} = 0.800 - 0.200\,k,
  \qquad k = 0,\ldots,4.
\]
\[
  \EA(k) = 0.625 \times \PDI(k) = 0.500 - 0.125\,k.
\]

\paragraph{Regime collapse.}
At $k = 2$: $\PDI = 0.400 < 0.5$.
The regime transitions from \emph{Flexible Planner} to
\emph{Linear Explorer}.
$\EA$ is halved from $0.500$ to $0.250$.

Results are confirmed by two certified bundles
(\texttt{1block}, \texttt{2block})
and depicted in Figure~\ref{fig:fragility}.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{PDI is not captured by existing metrics.}
$\AI$ measures how much of the state space is reachable;
$\PI$ measures how quickly that reach changes under perturbation.
Neither asks whether the reachable region is accessible via multiple
independent routes.
A system can have high $\AI$ (wide reach) and high $\PI$ (fast adaptation)
yet be entirely brittle under a single generator failure
(if $\PDI = 0$, every reachable state has exactly one path).
$\PDI$ and $\PI$ are independently necessary for $\EA$-maximising design.

\paragraph{The Merge Stability Criterion is a design target.}
Corollary~\ref{cor:merge} translates the abstract condition $\PDI > 0.5$
into a concrete engineering requirement:
at least two $\OK$-tagged generators must provide independent
merge edges to every target state.
Generator count alone is insufficient;
\emph{merge topology} --- the structure of reconvergent paths in the
condensation DAG --- is the decisive factor.

\paragraph{Certification chain.}
The obstruction bridge (\S\ref{sec:bridge}) closes a three-family
certification chain:
\[
  \text{Family [76] failure algebra}
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{tagging [86]}}\;
  \text{T2/T3 SCC bounds}
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{Theorem~\ref{thm:bridge}}}\;
  \PDI
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{Theorem~\ref{thm:decomp}}}\;
  \EA.
\]
$\PDI$ is derivable from the T3 path-propagation witness without
additional BFS computation.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The five competency metrics ($\AI, \PI, \GD, \CE, \PDI$) are defined
over generator graphs, not raw state-transition tables.
For systems with continuous or infinite state spaces, a discretised
generator graph is required; the $\PDI$ value will depend on the
resolution of discretisation.
Reference system values are hand-crafted exemplars illustrating framework
capabilities, not claims about specific wet-lab or deployed systems.

\section{Conclusion}

We have introduced $\PDI$, proved four structural theorems about its
behaviour, connected it to the QA failure algebra via the
PDI--Obstruction Bridge, and demonstrated both an existence proof
(FLEXIBLE PLANNER, $\PDI = 0.800$) and a fragility experiment
($\Delta\PDI = -0.200$ per PARITY\_BLOCK tag, regime collapse at $k=2$).

The central message:
\begin{quote}
  \emph{Flexible planning is not accidental.
  It requires merge topology with obstruction joins $= \OK$.
  Two PARITY\_BLOCK tags suffice to eliminate it.}
\end{quote}

All certified artefacts, figure generators, and reference bundles are
available at the repository tag
\texttt{family-26-figure2-fragility-v1.0.0}
\cite{QAFamily26Cert}.
