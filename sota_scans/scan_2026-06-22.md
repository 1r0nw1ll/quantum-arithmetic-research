## New Finds This Window

### 1. arXiv:2606.20439 — "Four-digit Kaprekar dynamics in odd bases"
**Authors:** Evan Chen, Ken Ono, Richard E. Schwartz, Dinesh S. Thakur  
**Submitted:** June 18, 2026 (math.NT + math.CO cross-listed)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20439

**Summary:** Kaprekar's routine: sort digits descending, sort ascending, subtract, repeat. Base 10 is famous for sending every nonconstant 4-digit string to 6174 (single attractor). This paper fully characterises the general odd-base case: in every odd base B > 3, after at most **three iterations** every nonconstant orbit enters an explicit **triangular region**, on which the map is conjugate to **projective doubling**. This yields a complete finite description of all terminal cycles: explicit formula for cycle lengths (longest ≤ (B−1)/2, with equality iff B is prime) and counts. AI-assisted: AxiomProver produced Lean/mathlib formalizations.

**QA Mapping Potential:**
- *Orbit tripartition analogy*: QA's three orbit classes (Cosmos 24-cycle / Satellite 8-cycle / Singularity 1-fixed) correspond structurally to the triangular-region attractor with multiple cycle lengths. Both systems: finite discrete map → orbit convergence → complete classification.
- *Projective doubling*: The conjugacy to projective doubling is structurally analogous to QA's step map conjugacy to arithmetic on Z/mZ. The "projective" structure in QA appears in the Whittaker direction set cert [266] and the SL(2,Z) cluster [294–296].
- *Explicit cycle count formula*: Parallels QA's 72/8/1 pair decomposition. Both produce exact closed-form orbit-size predictions from the arithmetic of the base.
- *Lean/mathlib formalization*: Adds to the Lean 4 ecosystem (arXiv:2605.20440 + arXiv:2604.13514 + SAIR ETP) for QA cert formalization pathway.
- *Caveats*: QA uses **even** moduli (24, 8, 9 for mod-9 theoretical) while this paper focuses on odd bases. The digit-manipulation operation is not QA's step function `((b+e-1)%m)+1`. Overlap is structural (orbit classification methodology, projective geometry appearance) not algebraic identity.

**Value rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**New cert candidate:** `qa_kaprekar_orbit_projective_cert_v1` — certify the structural analogy between QA's three-orbit classification theorem and the Kaprekar triangular-region convergence + projective doubling theorem. External validation of orbit tripartition principle from an independent discrete-map family.

### 2. arXiv:2512.05124 — "Information funnels and multiscale gap-space dynamics in Kaprekar's routine" ⚠️ MEGA-SLIP: all 13 scans
**Author:** Christoph D. Dahl  
**Submitted:** November 23, 2025 (math.DS)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.05124

**Summary:** Exhaustive analysis of Kaprekar's routine for digit-length D ∈ {3,4,5,6}: all states enumerated, attractors and convergence distances computed, "entropy funnels" constructed from the induced distribution over attractors across iterations. Despite combinatorial state-space growth, average convergence distances remain small and entropy decays rapidly. Gap-space reduction: states → digit multisets → low-dimensional digit-gap features, giving a first-order Markov approximation whose transition structure and stationary distribution are characterised. Gap features are strongly constraining for D=3 but lose predictive power as D grows.

**QA Mapping Potential:**
- *Entropy funnel*: Directly analogous to QA's orbit basin structure. The rapid entropy decay = Cosmos/Satellite pairs funneling into their respective periodic attractors. The gap-space Markov structure could provide information-theoretic grounding for QA's coupling dynamics.
- *Markov transition matrix on gap space*: QA's orbit transition dynamics (cert [455]) have a directly analogous structure — the Witt Tower orbit transition Markov chain cert operates on QA state bins in exactly this way.
- *Companion to arXiv:2606.20439*: Together the two papers give geometric (projective-doubling) + information-theoretic (entropy-funnel) dual perspectives on the same family of discrete maps.
