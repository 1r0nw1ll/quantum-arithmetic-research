### 1. arXiv:2512.25060 — On the geometry and topology of representations: the manifolds of modular addition
**Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH** (slipped **all 8 scans**; posted Dec 31, 2025)
**Authors:** Gabriela Moisescu-Pareja, Gavin McCracken, Harley Wiltzer, Vincent Létourneau, Colin Daniels, Doina Precup, Jonathan Love (McGill / Mila)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.25060

**Summary:** Investigates how neural networks with different architectures implement modular addition over Z/pZ. The central result is that all studied architectures — uniform attention, trainable attention, MLPs — learn preactivations that form topologically and geometrically equivalent manifolds, expressible in closed form. The methodology identifies all neurons corresponding to each representation and studies the collective as one topological entity. Introduces new TDA tools to validate topological equivalence conditions empirically. Main theorem: the learned representation manifold is universal, independent of architecture, for modular arithmetic tasks.

**QA Mapping Potential:** This paper directly validates QA's orbit topology claim at the representation level. QA's (b, e) state pairs over Z/24Z generate orbits that form topological objects; this paper proves that *any* computational system solving modular arithmetic over a cyclic group will converge to the same topological structure, independent of implementation. Three specific QA connections:
1. **Universal manifold → orbit invariant**: The paper's closed-form manifold for Z/pZ modular addition is directly applicable to QA's Z/24Z step map. QA's three orbit classes (24-cycle, 8-cycle, 1-cycle) correspond to three topologically distinct manifold components under this framework.
2. **TDA toolchain → orbit topology certification**: The TDA methods introduced here (homological equivalence validation for modular arithmetic representations) are exactly the tools needed for `qa_tda_orbit_grokking_cert_v1` (scan #4 scaffolding candidate). This paper provides the theoretical foundation and methodology for that cert.
3. **Architecture independence → Theorem NT support**: The fact that the representation manifold is uniquely determined by the algebraic structure (Z/pZ) rather than the implementation independently supports Theorem NT's claim that the observer projection layer recovers a fixed structure regardless of the continuous observer chosen.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** No new standalone cert needed. Add as primary reference to `qa_tda_orbit_grokking_cert_v1` (scan #4). Also directly grounds arXiv:2605.06352 (scan #4) at the representation level.

**Root cause of slip:** Search battery lacked "modular addition manifold", "topology of representations", or "TDA modular arithmetic". Add for scan #10.

### 2. arXiv:2605.24504 — Orbit decomposition statistics for discrete dynamical systems: the Cesàro mean and a large deviation principle
**Rating: MEDIUM** (slipped **scans #7–8**; posted May 23, 2026)
**Authors:** Gunther Cornelissen, Sun Woo Park (Utrecht)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24504

**Summary:** Proves a large deviation principle for the number of prime orbits occurring in the decomposition of a general orbit of length ≤ X, with speed B·log X and universal rate function equal to the Poisson distribution with unit mean (Theorem 1). The proof uses asymptotic results on the total number of general orbits (Cesàro mean formula) and a weak analogue of Mertens's second theorem. The framework applies to endomorphisms of algebraic groups over finite fields, additive cellular automata, and automorphisms of some solenoids.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The "endomorphisms of algebraic groups over finite fields" class includes QA's step map over Z/24Z. The prime orbit decomposition formula gives statistical laws for how many minimal-period sub-orbits appear within a general orbit of length ≤ X. For QA's Cosmos orbit (length 24 = 2³×3): the Mertens analogue predicts the count of prime orbits in the Z/24Z Cesàro mean sense. This is adjacent to but distinct from QA's exact orbit classification — QA has deterministic exact counts, while this paper gives the statistical distribution over all maps in the same algebraic family. The Cesàro mean result is the bridge: for the specific Z/24Z map, it becomes an exact statement.

Two caveats preventing HIGH rating: (1) QA's orbit classification is exact, not statistical — the large deviation principle is most useful when orbit structure is unknown; (2) the "prime orbit" concept (minimal-period orbit) differs from QA's orbit stratification (by period length). The paper nonetheless provides the most rigorous orbit counting framework for algebraic group endomorphisms published in 2026, making it the strongest statistical-mechanics grounding for QA's orbit count claims.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** No new cert. Add as statistical grounding reference to `qa_pisano_orbit_correspondence_cert_v1` [281] alongside arXiv:2510.24882.

### 3. arXiv:2506.05190 — Categorical foundations of discrete dynamical systems
**Rating: MEDIUM** (slipped **all 8 scans**; posted June 2025 — over 1 year old)
**Authors:** Daniel Carranza, Krzysztof Kapulkin, Nathan Kershaw, Reinhard Laubenbacher, Matthew Wheeler
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05190

**Summary:** Develops categorical foundations for discrete dynamical systems, aimed at understanding how system structure determines dynamics. Introduces the notion of a "cycle set" — a formal categorical language for describing system attractors. Proves a decomposition theorem that decomposes any discrete dynamical system into a canonical form with respect to its cycle set. Applied to Boolean networks, Petri nets, and cellular automata as running examples.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The cycle set concept formalizes exactly what QA's orbit stratification theorem asserts: a discrete dynamical system over a finite state space decomposes into a canonical attractor structure (the cycle set = the union of periodic orbits). For QA over Z/24Z: the cycle set has exactly three components (24-cycle Cosmos, 8-cycle Satellite, 1-cycle Singularity), and the decomposition theorem certifies this as the canonical attractor structure. The categorical formulation provides a language for stating QA's orbit stratification theorem formally, which is directly useful for the long-overdue `qa_orbit_stratification_cert_v1` [261]. However, this is a general framework paper rather than a QA-specific result — the cycle set of QA follows immediately from the axiom set and doesn't require the full categorical machinery.

**Why MEDIUM, not HIGH:** The categorical framework subsumes QA as a special case, but QA's orbit structure is elementary by comparison with the generality of the paper. The cert value is as a formal language reference, not a substantive structural result.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** Add as foundational reference to `qa_orbit_stratification_cert_v1` [261] (currently OCTUPLE GAP — this paper provides the categorical vocabulary for the human tract).

### 4. arXiv:2603.19343 — A Universal Identity for Powers in Quadratic Algebras
**Rating: MEDIUM** (slipped **scans #3–8**; posted March 2026)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19343

**Summary:** Proves a universal identity for powers of elements in 2D quadratic algebras: for any 2×2 matrix M with trace t and determinant d, the Cayley-Hamilton theorem gives M² = tM − dI, from which all powers of M can be expressed as integer-linear combinations of I and M with coefficients satisfying the Lucas-type recurrence. The paper derives Fibonacci and Lucas numbers as the special case t=1, d=−1, and gives a unified closed-form for generalized Fibonacci/Lucas sequences in terms of Chebyshev polynomials evaluated at t/2.

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's (b, e) → (b+e mod m, e) step is a 2×2 integer matrix action over Z/mZ. The characteristic polynomial of this matrix has trace t=2 and determinant d=1 (identity step), yielding period = ord(M) in GL(2, Z/mZ). The Cayley-Hamilton identity in this paper directly gives the recursion for powers of the QA step matrix, which determines orbit periods. This is the external algebraic identity underlying `qa_cayley_hamilton_fibonacci_lucas_cert_v1` [299] — the cert certifies a result that this paper proves as a general theorem. External grounding confirmed.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** No new cert. Add as primary reference to `qa_cayley_hamilton_fibonacci_lucas_cert_v1` [299].

### 6. arXiv:2604.08919 — Observing complementary Lucas sequences using non-Hermitian zero modes
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped **scans #1–8**; posted April 10, 2026)
**Author:** Li Ge (CUNY)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08919

**Summary:** Shows that complementary Lucas sequences (defined by the same Fibonacci recurrence a_{n+1} = a_n + a_{n-1} but with complementary initial conditions) can be observed in a gain-and-loss-modulated non-Hermitian resonator system. Linearly localized edge states manifest Fibonacci numbers; constant-intensity modes manifest Lucas numbers. Physical implementation: non-Hermitian reservoir bridging two mirror-symmetric systems.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Lucas sequences are generated by the same 2×2 matrix recurrence as Fibonacci sequences with complementary initial conditions (0,2) vs (0,1) in Lucas vs Fibonacci. In QA over Z/m: the two families of initializations correspond to orbits with different entry points to the same underlying cycle. The complementary Lucas/Fibonacci split in initial conditions maps to QA's orbit equivalence classes — pairs (b,e) that generate the same orbit period but differ in phase. However, the physical non-Hermitian implementation is orthogonal to QA's discrete arithmetic setting; the connection is at the level of the underlying linear recurrence, not QA's specific modular dynamics.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** None. Structural adjacency only.
