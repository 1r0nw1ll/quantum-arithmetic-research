## Levin 2019 — *The Computational Boundary of a "Self"*

**Journal:** Frontiers in Psychology 10:2688 (2019-12-13)
**On-disk:** `corpus/levin/computational_boundary_self_2019.pdf` (2.3MB, 1-author)
**Also known as:** "Cognitive Lightcone" paper (per OB 2026-04-06 mega-mapping)

### #self-as-epistemic-agent (p.1, Abstract)

> "All epistemic agents physically consist of parts that must somehow comprise an integrated cognitive self. Biological individuals consist of subunits (organs, cells, and molecular networks) that are themselves complex and competent in their own native contexts."

### #scale-free-cognition-hypothesis (p.1, Abstract)

> "I propose a fundamental definition of an Individual based on the ability to pursue goals at an appropriate level of scale and organization and suggest a formalism for defining and comparing the cognitive capacities of highly diverse types of agents."

### #computational-surface-definition (p.1, Abstract)

> "Any Self is demarcated by a computational surface – the spatio-temporal boundary of events that it can measure, model, and try to affect. This surface sets a functional boundary - a cognitive 'light cone' which defines the goals a system can pursue."

## Levin 2022 — *Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere (TAME)*

**Journal:** Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience 16:768201 (2022-03-24)
**On-disk:** `corpus/levin/technological_approach_mind_everywhere_2022.pdf` (15.6MB, 1-author)

### #tame-framework-definition (p.1, Abstract)

> "I introduce TAME—Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere—a framework for understanding and manipulating cognition in unconventional substrates. TAME formalizes a non-binary (continuous), empirically-based approach to strongly embodied agency."

### #deep-symmetry-problem-solving (p.1, Abstract)

> "The deep symmetry between problem-solving in anatomical, physiological, transcriptional, and 3D (traditional behavioral) spaces drives specific hypotheses by which cognitive capacities can increase during evolution."

## Pio-Lopez, Hartl & Levin 2026 — *BraiNCA: Brain-Inspired Neural Cellular Automata*

**Source:** arXiv:2604.01932
**On-disk:** `corpus/levin/brainca_2604.01932.pdf` (3.2MB, 3-author)

### #brainca-abstract

> "We introduce BraiNCA, a brain-inspired NCA with an attention layer, long-range connections and complex topology. BraiNCAs shows better results in terms of robustness and speed of learning on the two tasks compared to Vanilla NCAs establishing that incorporating attention-based message selection together with explicit long-range edges can yield more sample-efficient and damage-tolerant self-organization than purely local, grid-based update rules."
