2️⃣ Abstract
This research presents a spatial rendering framework based on Quantum Arithmetic (QA), enabling symbolic representation of space using canonical tuples and harmonic slicing. It introduces male and female ellipse structures, Diophantine tangency conditions, and modular overlays to form a complete geometric-cryptographic simulation system. The framework integrates tuple-based scaling, bisector rotation, and mod-n modular resonance, allowing precise field modeling and coordinate encoding. Applications span cryptography, wave physics, and symbolic simulation, offering a rigorous alternative to calculus-based systems.

3️⃣ Introduction
Quantum Arithmetic (QA) replaces traditional floating-point and calculus systems with symbolic integer-based logic derived from canonical tuples (b, e, d, a). Prior work in QA defines harmonic cycles, modular arithmetic, and resonance-based logic for physics, acoustics, and cryptography. This study addresses the spatial representation problem—how to encode and render arbitrary points, fields, and objects using QA alone. By rotating bisectors, scaling tuples, and applying modular overlays, this model constructs a harmonically accurate, cryptographically robust spatial engine.
