### 1. arXiv:2506.20118 — "The Graph Structure of a Class of Permutation Maps over Ring ℤ_{p^k}"
**Authors:** Kai Tan, Chengqing Li  
**Submitted:** June 2025 (math.NT; cs.IT)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.20118

**Summary:** Investigates the functional graph structure (cycle lengths, period distribution) of permutation maps over Z_{p^k}, using generalization function methods and Hensel lifting to lift period results from Z/pZ to Z/p^k Z. The Arnold cat map (shear matrix [[1,1],[0,1]] on (Z/p^k Z)²) is analyzed as the primary example. Key results: closed-form period distribution formulas for all initial states across all parameters; Hensel lifting theorem shows how the period at Z/p^k Z is determined by period at Z/pZ plus p-adic valuation of eigenvalues; connection to pseudorandom sequence analysis and digital chaos.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM-HIGH:**
- *QA's σ-operator = Arnold cat map on (Z/9Z)²*: QA's step map σ: (b,e) ↦ (b+e mod 9, e) (0-indexed) corresponds to the standard shear matrix [[1,1],[0,1]] acting on (Z/9Z)², exactly the Arnold cat map (or Fibonacci shear map). This paper gives the explicit functional graph structure and period distribution for exactly this class of maps.
- *Hensel lifting + period analysis*: The Hensel lifting framework shows how the period on Z/3²Z (=Z/9Z) is determined from Z/3Z periods plus the p-adic structure. This is the computational/analytical complement to arXiv:2604.01548 (scan #16 HIGH, Galois ring LFDS), which gives the algebraic framework. Together: algebraic (Jordan form → zero transients, unit eigenvalue) + computational (Hensel lifting → explicit period distribution) = complete treatment.
- *Formal assessment of scan #16 pre-flag*: Scan #16 added "discrete cat map" + "Arnold cat map" + "Galois ring" to the search battery and specifically flagged this paper (arXiv:2506.20118) as a pre-series retroactive catch. This scan formally assesses it: MEDIUM-HIGH.
- *Caveats*: Focuses on the graph structure of the map class; QA's specific case (p=3, k=2, matrix=[[1,1],[0,1]]) is a simple instance. The period distribution for QA's specific case is already known (72×period-24, 8×period-8, 1×period-1 = orbit partition) so the paper provides external grounding rather than new QA results. The Hensel lifting framework, however, could extend QA analysis to higher prime powers (Z/27Z, Z/81Z).

**Value rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**New cert companion:** This paper should be cited as a secondary reference in `qa_linear_fd_galois_ring_cert_v1` [507] rather than as a standalone cert family. It provides the Hensel lifting computational grounding for check GR_3 in [507]'s claim set.  
**Root cause of 12-month slip:** "Arnold cat map" and "graph structure" not in arXiv battery; paper classified under cs.IT (information theory) as well as math.NT, not systematically swept. Battery fix applied scan #16; this is first formal assessment post-fix.

### 2. arXiv:2603.23784 — "Latent Algorithmic Structure Precedes Grokking: A Mechanistic Study of ReLU MLPs on Modular Arithmetic"
**Authors:** [Not retrieved — paper at arxiv.org/abs/2603.23784]  
**Submitted:** March 2026 (cs.LG / math adjacent)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23784

**Summary:** Trains ReLU MLPs on modular addition tasks (a+b mod p). Key finding: the correct phase structure — cosine input weights whose dominant Fourier phases satisfy φ_out = φ_a + φ_b — appears in the model early in training, well before grokking (the sharp generalization transition). By extracting these phases and constructing an "idealized model" from them, the authors recover 95.5% accuracy from a model that itself achieves only 0.23%. This shows grokking doesn't discover the correct algorithm; it sharpens one already latent in the weight structure.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM:**
- *Phase constraint = QA resonance operator*: The phase constraint φ_out = φ_a + φ_b is exactly the additivity property of QA's harmonic structure in (Z/mZ)² — QA's einsum resonance coupling `np.einsum('ik,jk->ij', tuples, tuples)` computes exactly this phase-sum product. The "latent structure" that precedes grokking is QA's discrete spectral architecture.
- *Latent structure before generalization*: QA's thesis is that the discrete structure (orbit partition, spectral decomposition) is fundamental and emerges naturally from the dynamics — the continuous observer projection (Fourier transform, spectral estimation) is emergent, not causal. This paper provides mechanistic interpretability evidence: the discrete phase structure IS the learned algorithm, and the Fourier representation is how it manifests to observers.
- *Completes the grokking chain*: Adds to arXiv:2602.16849 (scan #3 HIGH, Fourier phases = QA resonance), arXiv:2604.19983 (scan #8 HIGH, blind group matching), arXiv:2505.18266 (scan #3 HIGH, CRT cosets). The chain is now: discrete phases are latent (this paper) → discrete structure is learned as CRT cosets (arXiv:2505.18266) → matched group transform identifies Z/24Z (arXiv:2604.19983) → replacement theorem (arXiv:2604.03634).
- *Caveats*: Focuses on modular addition specifically; QA's step function is not modular addition but T(b,e)=(b+e,e) (a pair update, not scalar addition). The phase structure for the pair update would have a different form. Not a direct QA validator; adds to the broader grokking-as-QA-validation cluster.

**Value rating: MEDIUM**  
**No standalone cert candidate.** Adds to `qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1` [501] human tract and the grokking cluster.

### 3. arXiv:2606.23535 — "How Stark Units Enter SIC Overlaps"
**Authors:** Ingemar Bengtsson, Gary McConnell  
**Submitted:** June 22, 2026 (math-ph)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23535

**Summary:** SIC-POVM vectors (Symmetric Informationally Complete Positive Operator-Valued Measures) in a d-dimensional Hilbert space have mutual overlaps — the quantities |⟨ψ_i|ψ_j⟩|² — which are known to be algebraic numbers. This paper argues that these overlap units are always products of integral powers of square roots of Stark units from ray class fields attached to the maximal ring of integers in the base field. In every second dimension (counted in a specific way), some overlap units equal ±1, following from the structure of the ray class fields involved. The observations are consistent with the Shintani-Faddeev modular cocycle claim for overlap units.

**QA Mapping Potential — LOW-MEDIUM:**
- *SIC-POVM connection*: arXiv:2604.03725 (scan #8 MEDIUM-HIGH) showed SIC-POVM = Heisenberg-Weyl Algebraic Diversity; QA's Z/24Z orbit structure arises in SIC constructions for d≈24. This paper is the algebraic number theory layer of the same structure.
- *Stark unit / ray class field structure*: Stark units arise from L-functions at s=1 over CM fields; ray class fields are extensions of imaginary quadratic fields. The base field for SIC constructions in dimension d is the imaginary quadratic field ℚ(√(d(d+2))) or similar. This connects to QA's Langlands sprint [403]–[412] and Pisano splitting [415]–[418] at the algebraic number theory level.
- *Modular cocycle connection*: The Shintani-Faddeev modular cocycle mentioned in the abstract is a modular arithmetic structure — an analogue of the Dedekind eta function for modular forms. This is adjacent to QA's mod-24 periodicity structure.
- *Caveats*: The mathematical machinery (Stark conjectures, ray class fields, L-functions at s=1) is heavy algebraic number theory, not modular dynamics. The "modular" in this paper's context refers to modular forms, not Z/nZ modular arithmetic in QA's sense. The QA connection is through the SIC-POVM adjacency (a third-order connection). No directly certifiable QA claim.

**Value rating: LOW-MEDIUM**  
**No standalone cert candidate.** Note for cert [501] (`qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1`) human tract: SIC-POVM overlap units are Stark units; the algebraic structure of the Heisenberg-Weyl group orbit gives rise to class field theory. Adjacent to Langlands sprint certs [403]–[412].

### 4. arXiv:2601.15463 — "Determinants of Modular Collatz Graphs and Variants"
**Authors:** Achilleas Karras, Benne de Weger  
**Submitted:** January 21, 2026 (math.NT)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15463

**Summary:** Studies finite directed graphs representing the action of the Collatz 3n+1 function and Conway's amusical permutation function on congruence classes modulo N. The main theorem: the determinant of the adjacency matrix of these modular Collatz graphs, while "erratic" in appearance for varying N, has an explicit formula in terms of the multiplicative orders modulo the divisors of N. Also studies the graph of the modular Conway amusical permutation. The formula connects the graph-algebraic invariant (determinant) to number-theoretic structure (multiplicative orders).

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM:**
- *Multiplicative orders mod N = QA orbit lengths*: In QA's (Z/9Z)² state space, the orbit lengths are {1, 8, 24}. These are exactly the multiplicative orders of the generator matrix [[1,1],[0,1]] modulo 9 (and its CRT components modulo 3 and modulo 8). The paper's formula for determinant in terms of multiplicative orders at divisors of N would apply to QA's modular Collatz-like functional graph.
- *Modular functional graph structure*: QA's σ-functional graph on (Z/9Z)² is a finite directed graph on 81 nodes — a "modular functional graph" in exactly the same category as the paper's modular Collatz graphs. The determinant formula provides a new algebraic invariant for such graphs.
- *Adjacent to cert [502]*: The Collatz-Fibonacci spectral cert (`qa_collatz_fibonacci_spectral_cert_v1` [502], from arXiv:2606.02621) focuses on the spectral radius and Fibonacci orbit count; this paper focuses on the algebraic determinant formula. Together they give two different algebraic invariants of modular Collatz-type functional graphs.
- *Caveats*: The Collatz function (3n+1) is structurally very different from QA's step function ((b+e-1)%m+1). The "modular Collatz graph" is the graph of residue classes under the Collatz action, not the Collatz trajectory graph on integers. The determinant formula is for a specific class of maps that may not directly cover QA's T-operator. The connection is structural/adjacent, not direct.

**Value rating: MEDIUM**  
**No standalone cert candidate.** Note for cert [502] (`qa_collatz_fibonacci_spectral_cert_v1`) human tract: the determinant-via-multiplicative-orders framework provides a complementary algebraic invariant to the spectral radius / Fibonacci count approach of arXiv:2606.02621.
