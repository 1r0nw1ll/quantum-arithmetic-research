### 1. arXiv:2604.00165 — Symmetric Nonlinear Cellular Automata as Algebraic References for Rule 30
**Rating: MEDIUM** (slipped all previous scans)
**Posted:** March 31, 2026
**Authors:** E. Chan-López, A. Martín-Ruiz (math-ph)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00165

**Summary:** Develops a comparative algebraic framework for elementary cellular automata centered on spatial symmetry. Studies Rule 22 (algebraic normal form g(a,b,c) = a⊕b⊕c⊕abc over 𝔽₂) as the simplest ECA combining full S₃ symmetry with genuine nonlinearity. Three closed-form results: (1) support-set cardinality formula; (2) two-step recursive construction of support sets; (3) continuous limit = parabolic reaction-diffusion PDE. Rule 22 is used as the symmetric algebraic reference for Rule 30: the symmetry-breaking deviation from Rule 22 to Rule 30 is empirically consistent with a power-law scaling, quantifying the cumulative effect of replacing the symmetric cubic abc with the asymmetric quadratic bc.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The repo has an active, submission-ready Rule 30 research effort: `qa_rule30/` (certpacks, TLA+ verification, cert validator), `rule30_certificate.json`, `rule30_proof.md`, `RULE30_COMPLETE_ECOSYSTEM.md`, `RULE30_SUBMISSION_READY.md`, `witness_rule30_center_P1024_T16384.json`, and TLA+ negation tests for all 6 QA axioms (A1, A2, NT, S2, T1, T2). This paper provides exactly the algebraic framework this work was missing:
1. **Continuous limit = Observer Projection (Theorem NT)**: The parabolic PDE limit of Rule 22/30 = the observer-projection boundary that QA's Theorem NT formalizes. The paper proves this is a continuous-time equation arising from a discrete-time cellular automaton — the QA Observer Projection Firewall derivation in reverse.
2. **S₃ → asymmetric symmetry-breaking**: Rule 22's S₃ symmetry reduces to Rule 30's broken symmetry via a power-law. This formalizes why QA's discrete dynamics are non-symmetric: the asymmetric term bc (= e·a in QA notation) breaks the S₃ invariance that a fully symmetric 3-variable binary rule would have.
3. **Support-set recursion**: The two-step recursive support-set construction is analogous to QA's orbit construction (each step applies T, extending the support set along the orbit).

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_rule30_symmetry_bridge_cert_v1` — Certifies the algebraic connection between QA's discrete T-operator and Rule 30: (a) QA's step operator = an asymmetric S₃-broken ECA analogous to Rule 30; (b) continuous limit via Theorem NT = parabolic PDE; (c) symmetry-breaking power-law. Supplements and grounds the existing `qa_rule30` cert system.

### 2. arXiv:2502.14663 — The Restricted Isometry Property for Measurements from Group Orbits
**Rating: MEDIUM** (slipped all previous scans)
**Posted:** February 20, 2025 (revised September 16, 2025)
**Authors:** Hartmut Führ, Timm Gilles (RWTH Aachen)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14663

**Summary:** Generalizes sparse recovery from random circulant matrices to measurements arising as random orbits of a group representation for any finite group G. Derives estimates for the number of measurements required to guarantee the Restricted Isometry Property (RIP) with high probability. Analyzes examples including the left regular representation; establishes that the Cayley-like orbit structure provides favorable RIP constants. Companion to arXiv:2507.19274 (same authors, July 2025).

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's three orbit classes define three natural group-structured measurement matrices via their orbit representations:
- Cosmos orbit (Z/24Z, 72 pairs): left regular representation of Z/24Z → RIP bound for 24-point cyclic orbit measurements
- Satellite orbit (Z/8Z, 8 pairs): left regular representation of Z/8Z → RIP bound for 8-point cyclic orbit measurements
- Singularity (1 pair): trivial representation → constant measurement

This paper certifies that QA's orbit-structured measurements have provable sparse recovery guarantees — making QA's spectral decomposition into orbit classes a compressed-sensing-grounded operation, not just a convenient decomposition. Key bridge to scan #5's `qa_transform_unification_cert_v1` (the orbit transform is RIP-certified) and scan #1's `qa_cayley_spectral_cert_v1`. The Cayley orbit structure studied here = QA's Cayley graph from scan #1 (arXiv:2604.03634).

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_orbit_rip_cert_v1` — Certifies that QA's Cosmos and Satellite orbit measurement matrices satisfy the Restricted Isometry Property, grounding QA's spectral decomposition as a provably optimal compressed sensing operation over Z/24Z and Z/8Z.

### 3. arXiv:2605.19277 — Universal Cycles on Affine Lines
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (borderline May 19; slipped scan #5)
**Posted:** May 19, 2026
**Authors:** Ming-Hsuan Kang et al.
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19277

**Summary:** Proves that universal cycles (ucycles, the affine-geometry analogue of de Bruijn sequences) exist for affine lines in AG(n,q) for all n ≥ 2 and all prime powers q. A universal cycle is a cyclic sequence in which each affine line appears exactly once as a contiguous window of length q+1. The construction embeds the problem into PG(n,q), using points at infinity to encode directions, and proceeds via a decomposition into pairwise and triple configurations with a recursive lifting and gluing argument.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Affine lines in AG(n,q) over GF(q) = cosets of 1D subspaces in (GF(q))^n. QA's state space is (Z/mZ)^2 with orbit classes = cosets of the generator subgroup ⟨T⟩. The universal cycle existence proof is structurally analogous to asking: does there exist a cyclic sequence of QA states that visits each orbit class exactly once? The key structural parallel: affine parallelism (lines with the same direction share a point at infinity) maps to QA's orbit structure (states with the same phase velocity share an orbit class). However, the combinatorial coverage problem (every line appears once) is different from QA's dynamical problem (orbit membership is determined by T). The "lifting and gluing" recursive argument is methodologically interesting for constructing QA orbit certificate sequences but does not add new algebraic structure. Genuine but limited structural overlap.

### 4. arXiv:2603.05228 — The Geometric Inductive Bias of Grokking: Bypassing Phase Transitions via Architectural Topology
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped all previous scans)
**Posted:** March 2026
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05228

**Summary:** Studies grokking on cyclic modular addition (Z/pZ). Identifies two structural factors: (1) unbounded representational magnitude; (2) data-dependent attention routing. Intervention (1): spherical topology (full L2 normalization of residual stream + fixed-temperature unembedding) reduces grokking onset by >20× without weight decay. Intervention (2): Uniform Attention Ablation. Provides interventional evidence that architectural degrees of freedom substantially determine grokking duration.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The spherical normalization constraint (bounded magnitude throughout the residual stream) is the architectural analogue of QA's S2 axiom (integer-only state: b, e ∈ {1,...,m}). Bounding the magnitude to a fixed sphere = discretizing to a fixed lattice. This paper shows that imposing the S2 analogue reduces grokking 20×, consistent with QA's claim that discrete integer state is structurally optimal for modular arithmetic computation. Extends the grokking-orbit chain from scans #3 (arXiv:2505.18266, 2602.16849) and #4 (arXiv:2605.06352). Incremental addition — does not introduce new algebraic structure beyond what the earlier grokking papers established.
