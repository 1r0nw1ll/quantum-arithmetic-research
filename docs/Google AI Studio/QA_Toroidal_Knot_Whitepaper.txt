\begin{abstract}
We present a rigorous synthesis of Greg Volk’s toroidal knot and vortex topology with the number-theoretic structure of Quantum Arithmetic (QA). By mapping Volk’s $m:n$ toroidal winding ratios and quantum jumps into QA’s canonical $(b, e, d, a)$ tuples and five digital root harmonic sets, we construct a unified field model that classifies particle-like knots, harmonic ellipses, and photon transitions. Our approach uses modular arithmetic, recursive tuples, and geometric embeddings to connect curvature and torsion to QA algebra, embedding knot structures in projective and toroidal forms. These results enhance particle modeling, modular topologies, and cryptographic systems.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Topological knot models of field structures—especially on toroidal surfaces—provide elegant, discrete, and recursive tools to describe stable particle-like systems. Greg Volk’s work in toroidal coordinates and vortex theory introduces a framework to encode quantum jumps, electromagnetic topology, and harmonic resonance. Quantum Arithmetic (QA), developed by Iverson and expanded by Pond and Elkins, offers an integer-based recursive system governed by prime distributions and digital root cycles.

This paper synthesizes Volk’s toroidal theory with QA’s arithmetic logic by establishing valid mappings between toroidal windings and QA tuples, classifying their harmonic families, and embedding them geometrically via mod-24 and projective tori.

\section{Toroidal Closure and QA Tuple Mapping}
Volk defines stable particles as $m:n$ toroidal windings, with knot closure governed by $\gcd(m, n) = 1$. QA’s canonical tuples obey:
\[
d = b + e, \quad a = d + e \Rightarrow a = b + 2e
\]
Valid toroidal embeddings require $\gcd(a, d) = 1$.
