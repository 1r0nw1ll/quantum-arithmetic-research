### Physical grounding for Theorem NT

In linear elasticity, longitudinal and transverse modes are orthogonal eigenmodes of the wave equation with different propagation speeds (P-waves vs S-waves). A pure compression wave cannot generate shear — structural impossibility, not convention.

Theorem NT is the QA expression of this mode orthogonality: the same tuple space supports both discrete generators and continuous observers, but they are structurally separated — mixing them would be like turning a P-wave into an S-wave.
