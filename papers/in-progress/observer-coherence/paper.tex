\begin{abstract}
We introduce the \emph{QA Coherence Index} (QCI), a structural indicator
derived from the transition dynamics of Quantum Arithmetic (QA), and show
it detects system instability across unrelated domains. The QCI measures
the rolling prediction accuracy of the QA Fibonacci-shift operator applied
to topographically clustered multi-channel signals. In finance, a local
observer produces QCI that anticorrelates with future volatility (partial
$r = -0.22$ beyond lagged realized volatility, 84\% robustness), while a
separate global observer shows the opposite sign ($r = +0.25$); their gap
achieves partial $r = -0.42$ (100\% robustness). Cross-domain validation
in EEG seizure classification ($\Delta R^2 = +0.19$, 15 patients),
audio signal analysis (partial $r = +0.75$), ERA5 atmospheric reanalysis
(partial $r = +0.43$), and climate teleconnection ($\chi^2 = 95$ for ENSO
phase discrimination) demonstrates the architecture is domain-general.
Process-level surrogate validation with a corrected null design ---
holding real targets fixed while replacing only QCI with
process-matched surrogates --- confirms four of six domains exceed
surrogate baselines. The core finding: deviation from QA-predicted
transition dynamics carries forward-looking structural information that
domain-specific baselines do not fully capture.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The problem of detecting structural regime changes in complex systems ---
financial markets, neural dynamics, acoustic signals --- is central to
multiple scientific disciplines. Classical approaches rely on domain-specific
features: realized volatility in finance, spectral power ratios in
neuroscience, autocorrelation in signal processing. While effective within
their domains, these features lack a unifying mathematical framework that
explains \emph{why} they work and \emph{what structural property} they
measure.

Quantum Arithmetic (QA) is a modular dynamical system operating on integer
state pairs $(b,e) \in \{1,\ldots,m\}^2$ via the Fibonacci-shift operator
$T(b,e) = (e, ((b+e-1) \bmod m) + 1)$. The state space partitions into
three orbit types: \emph{singularity} (period 1, fixed point),
\emph{satellite} (period 8 for $m=24$), and \emph{cosmos} (period 24).
This partition is a discrete analogue of the triune vibratory structure
described by Keely~\cite{keely} and formalized in Sympathetic Vibratory Physics.

We propose that QA provides the missing unifying framework: continuous
observables enter at the \emph{observer boundary}, are projected into discrete
QA states, and the coherence of subsequent state transitions --- measured by
the T-operator's prediction accuracy --- constitutes a domain-general
structural indicator.

\subsection{Contributions}

The paper makes three empirical claims supported by a unifying principle:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\textbf{A.}] \textbf{Local QCI detects path-law breakdown.}
    A local topographic observer (multi-channel returns) produces QCI via
    T-operator prediction accuracy. Low local QCI anticorrelates with future
    volatility (partial $r = -0.22$ beyond lagged RV, 84\% robustness,
    permutation-validated).
  \item[\textbf{B.}] \textbf{Global QCI detects manifold contraction.}
    A separate global observer (correlation, PCA concentration, dispersion)
    produces QCI with the \emph{opposite sign}: high global QCI correlates
    with future volatility ($r = +0.25$). During stress, the coupling
    manifold \emph{contracts} (everything correlates).
  \item[\textbf{C.}] \textbf{QCI gap detects stress as structural divergence.}
    The discrepancy $\text{QCI}_{\text{gap}} = \text{QCI}_{\text{local}} -
    \text{QCI}_{\text{global}}$ achieves partial $r = -0.42$ beyond lagged RV
    at best configuration, with 100\% robustness (16/16 grid) and
    permutation-validated ($p < 0.001$). Stress is the regime where the
    market becomes globally more constrained while locally less lawful.
\end{enumerate}

Cross-domain validation in EEG ($\Delta R^2 = +0.19$, 15 patients),
audio (partial $r = +0.75$), ERA5 atmospheric reanalysis (partial $r = +0.43$),
climate teleconnection ($\chi^2 = 95$ for ENSO phase discrimination), and
cardiac arrhythmia ($\Delta R^2 = +0.037$ beyond R-R intervals, pre-registered
$\Phi(D)$ confirmed) demonstrates the architecture is domain-general.
Process-level surrogate validation with corrected null design confirms five
of seven domains at Tier~3 (predictive, beyond process-matched baselines).

\section{Background: Quantum Arithmetic}
\label{sec:qa}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\begin{definition}[QA State Space]
For modulus $m$, the QA state space is $S_m = \{1,\ldots,m\}^2$.
The Fibonacci-shift operator $T: S_m \to S_m$ is defined by
\[
  T(b,e) = \bigl(e,\; ((b+e-1) \bmod m) + 1\bigr).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[QA Norm]
The QA norm $f: S_m \to \mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z}$ is
$f(b,e) = b^2 + be - e^2 \pmod{m}$.
This is the norm form of the golden ratio ring $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]$~\cite{iverson2}.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Orbit Partition]
For $m = 9$, $S_9$ partitions into exactly three orbit families:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Singularity}: 1 state $(9,9)$, period 1
  \item \textbf{Satellite}: 8 states, period 8, symmetric 3D structure
  \item \textbf{Cosmos}: 72 states, period 24, linear 1D structure
\end{itemize}
Total: $1 + 8 + 72 = 81 = 9^2$. The orbit period 24 equals the
Pisano period $\pi(9)$~\cite{wall1960}.
\end{proposition}

\section{The Topographic Observer Architecture}
\label{sec:observer}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Observer-Projection Firewall (Theorem NT)}

Continuous measurements enter QA \emph{exactly once} at the observer boundary
and exit \emph{exactly once} at the projection boundary. No continuous
quantity participates in QA dynamics. This firewall is both a correctness
constraint and a design principle.

\subsection{Pipeline}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{fig2_pipeline.pdf}
\caption{The topographic observer pipeline. Continuous signals enter at
the observer boundary (left), are discretized via $k$-means, mapped to
QA states, and the T-operator prediction accuracy is projected back as
the continuous QCI signal (right). No continuous quantity participates
in QA dynamics (center).}
\label{fig:pipeline}
\end{figure}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Observer layer} (continuous permitted):
    Multi-channel signal $\mathbf{x}_t \in \mathbb{R}^d$ is clustered into
    $K$ discrete microstates via $k$-means~\cite{kmeans}: $c_t = \text{cluster}(\mathbf{x}_t)$.
  \item \textbf{QA layer} (integer only):
    Microstate transitions map to QA states via a designed mapping
    $\phi: \{0,\ldots,K{-}1\}^2 \to S_m$:
    $(b_t, e_t) = \phi(c_t, c_{t+1})$.
  \item \textbf{T-operator prediction}:
    $(\hat{b}_{t+1}, \hat{e}_{t+1}) = T(b_t, e_t)$.
  \item \textbf{Projection layer} (continuous permitted):
    Rolling prediction accuracy over window $W$:
    \[
      \text{QCI}_t = \frac{1}{W} \sum_{s=t-W+1}^{t}
        \mathbf{1}[\hat{e}_{s+1} = e_{s+1}^{\text{actual}}]
    \]
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Mapping Design}

The microstate-to-QA mapping $\phi$ must ensure all three orbit types
(singularity, satellite, cosmos) are reachable from the microstate transition
set. For $m = 24$, satellite requires multiples of 8; singularity requires
$(24,24)$. Our canonical mapping for $K=6$:
$\{0 \mapsto 8,\; 1 \mapsto 16,\; 2 \mapsto 24,\; 3 \mapsto 5,\;
4 \mapsto 3,\; 5 \mapsto 11\}$.

\section{Cross-Domain Validation}
\label{sec:cross}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{EEG Seizure Classification}

Using 23-channel EEG recordings from the CHB-MIT Scalp EEG Database~\cite{chbmit}
(15 patients), a topographic $k$-means observer classifies 10-second windows
into 4 microstates. QA orbit distributions differ significantly between
seizure and baseline: mean $\Delta R^2 = +0.19$, Fisher combined
$p = 7.4 \times 10^{-44}$ via Fisher's method~\cite{fisher1932},
15/15 patients with positive $\Delta R^2$.

The pattern: baseline EEG maps to singularity-dominated states (stable
resting topography), while seizure maps to cosmos-dominated states
(dynamic propagation). This mirrors the finance finding: instability
corresponds to departure from the singularity/dominant mode.

\subsection{Audio Signal Structure}

QA orbit transition rates differ by signal type: sine waves at 880~Hz
show elevated orbit return rates ($0.17$ vs $0.11$ chance), while white
noise matches the null distribution. Partial $r = +0.75$ ($p = 0.02$)
beyond lag-1 autocorrelation confirms QA structure is real, though the
effect is modest.

\subsection{Seismology: Earthquake Catalog}

Using the USGS earthquake catalog~\cite{usgs} (152,237 M4+ events, 2016--2026),
daily features are computed: event count, mean magnitude, max magnitude,
and mean depth (4 channels). The topographic observer ($K=4$, mod-24)
applied to these features produces QCI that correlates with future
21-day seismic activity ($r = +0.23$, $p < 10^{-8}$, $n = 1679$ OOS days).

Orbit distributions differ significantly between active and quiet periods
($\chi^2 = 61.3$, $p < 10^{-6}$):
active periods show elevated satellite (60\%) and singularity (4\%)
rates, while quiet periods are cosmos-dominated (60\%).
This mirrors the finance pattern: instability corresponds to departure
from cosmos toward bounded/singularity orbits.

\subsection{ERA5 Atmospheric Reanalysis}

Using WeatherBench2~\cite{weatherbench2} ERA5 daily reanalysis (2014--2023), 15 channels
(geopotential, temperature, u-wind at 500~hPa across 5 global grid points),
the topographic observer ($K=6$, mod-24, $W=63$) produces QCI that
correlates with future 21-day atmospheric variability
($r = +0.46$, $p < 10^{-8}$, $n = 1538$ OOS days).
Partial correlation controlling lagged variability:
$r_{\text{partial}} = +0.43$ ($p < 10^{-8}$).
This is the \emph{strongest} cross-domain QCI result.

\subsection{Climate Teleconnection Indices}

Five NOAA~\cite{noaa} climate indices (ONI, NAO, AO, PDO, AMO; 1950--2023, 876 months)
form the input channels. While QCI does not significantly predict future
cross-index dispersion (partial $r = -0.13$, $p = 0.007$), the orbit
distribution differs dramatically by ENSO phase
($\chi^2 = 94.9$, $p < 10^{-19}$, OOS):
La~Ni\~{n}a maps to 97\% satellite orbits (bounded oscillation),
El~Ni\~{n}o shows 39\% cosmos (expansive dynamics).
This structural discrimination survives all four surrogate types
(Section~\ref{sec:surrogates}), confirming it is QA-specific.

\subsection{Cardiac ECG: Pre-Registered $\Phi(D)$ Test}

Using the MIT-BIH Arrhythmia Database~\cite{chbmit} (48 records, 94,536
annotated beats), beat-level features (8 morphological + 2 R-R interval
features per beat) are clustered into 4 microstates. QA orbit statistics
(singularity and cosmos fractions over 20-beat windows) are computed and
tested as additional predictors of arrhythmia beyond R-R interval alone.

This domain was \emph{pre-registered} as disorder-stress ($\Phi = -1$):
arrhythmia disrupts normal sinus rhythm. The prediction was written before
any cardiac QCI results were computed.

Result: $\Delta R^2 = +0.037$ ($p < 10^{-6}$) beyond R-R interval baseline.
Both surrogate types beaten at $p < 0.001$ (200 iterations each).
The pre-registered domain classification is confirmed: QA orbit features
detect arrhythmia structure that R-R intervals alone do not capture.

\section{Process-Level Surrogate Validation}
\label{sec:surrogates}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{The Circular Null Problem}

Na\"ive surrogate designs generate surrogate data and then derive
\emph{both} the QCI predictor and the target variable from the surrogate.
This creates a circular null: temporally smooth surrogates trivially produce
correlated predictors and targets, yielding false negatives when the
real signal fails to exceed the inflated surrogate baseline.

We identify this as a general methodological hazard: any surrogate test
that lets the null hypothesis generate its own success criterion will be
biased against the real signal.

\subsection{Corrected Design}

Building on the surrogate data framework of Theiler et~al.~\cite{theiler1992}
and Schreiber and Schmitz~\cite{schreiber2000}, the corrected test holds
the \emph{real target} fixed and replaces only the predictor:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Compute the real target (future volatility, seizure labels, ENSO phases)
    from real data. This is held fixed across all surrogates.
  \item Generate surrogate data (phase-randomized, AR(1), block-shuffled, or
    label-permuted) and derive surrogate QCI/orbit features from it.
  \item Compare: does QCI from real data predict the real target better than
    QCI from surrogate data?
\end{enumerate}

This asks the correct question: ``Does QA-derived structure in the real
signal carry information that process-matched random signals do not?''

For the audio domain, where only 9 test signals exist, we use
\emph{encoding surrogates}: the real signals and their properties (lag-1
autocorrelation, group labels) are held fixed, and the QA mod-9 prediction
rule is replaced with random lookup tables, shifted modular rules,
alternative moduli, or permuted state assignments.

\section{The Local/Global Observer Separation}
\label{sec:separation}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Not all continuous observables should be naively stacked into the same
clustering space. We distinguish:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Local state observables} (returns, RV, volume): describe the
    system's current coordinates. Safe to stack.
  \item \textbf{Global structure observables} (correlation, PCA eigenstructure,
    entropy): describe the geometry of the state space itself. Require a
    separate observer layer.
\end{itemize}

When cross-asset correlation was naively added as an 8th channel, the QCI
sign \emph{reversed} --- the correlation channel dominated clustering and
redefined what microstates represent. This is a \texttt{LOCAL\_GLOBAL\_LAYER\_COLLISION}
failure: a manifold descriptor was treated as a coordinate, changing the
semantic meaning of QCI without modeling it as a separate layer.

The correct architecture is a \emph{multi-layer observer}: local and global
observers produce separate QCI signals, and their discrepancy (the gap
between local coherence and global coherence) is where regime-change
information lives.

Empirical validation confirms this architecture. A pure global observer
using rolling correlation, PCA concentration, and dispersion features
produces $\text{QCI}_{\text{global}}$ with $r = +0.25$ with future volatility
(note the \emph{opposite sign} from local QCI). The gap signal
$\text{QCI}_{\text{gap}} = \text{QCI}_{\text{local}} - \text{QCI}_{\text{global}}$
achieves partial $r = -0.42$ beyond lagged RV at its best configuration ---
nearly double the local-only signal. Robustness: 16/16 grid configurations
significant for both global and gap signals (100\%).

The opposite signs carry the key insight: during stress, the coupling
structure \emph{tightens} (correlation increases $\to$ high global coherence)
while individual trajectories \emph{fragment} (chaotic movement $\to$ low
local coherence). The discrepancy between these layers --- structural
contraction with trajectory disorder --- is the universal stress signature.

\section{The QA Observable Principle}
\label{sec:principle}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We now state the theoretical principle underlying the empirical results.

\begin{definition}[QA Observer System]
A \emph{QA observer system} is a triple $(S_m, G, \pi)$ where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $S_m = \{1,\ldots,m\}^2$ is the QA state space;
  \item $G = \{T\}$ is the generator set (Fibonacci shift, possibly extended
    with $\sigma, \mu, \lambda, \nu$);
  \item $\pi: S_m \to \mathcal{O}$ is an observer projection into a
    continuous observation space $\mathcal{O}$.
\end{itemize}
The \emph{reachability graph} $\Gamma_G$ has vertex set $S_m$ and directed
edges $(s, T(s))$ for each generator $T \in G$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[QA Observable Principle]
\label{thm:observable}
Let $(S_m, G, \pi)$ be a QA observer system. Then observed data
$o_0, o_1, \ldots$ are not primitive objects but arise as pushforwards of
path measures on $(S_m, \Gamma_G)$:
\[
  P_k(o \mid s_0) = \mu_k\bigl(\pi^{-1}(o)\bigr),
\]
where $\mu_k$ is a measure on states reachable from $s_0$ along paths of
length $k$ in $\Gamma_G$.

The \emph{QA Coherence Index} at time $t$ is the empirical estimate of
the probability that observed transitions conform to $\Gamma_G$:
\[
  \mathrm{QCI}_t = \frac{1}{W}\sum_{s=t-W+1}^{t}
    \mathbf{1}\bigl[(b_{s+1}, e_{s+1}) \in \Gamma_G(b_s, e_s)\bigr].
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[QCI as Manifold Deviation]
\label{prop:deviation}
Low QCI indicates that observed transitions frequently leave the admissible
edges of $\Gamma_G$. If the system's true dynamics are well-approximated
by QA generators, then low QCI signals departure from structural equilibrium.
Empirically, this manifests as:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item anticorrelation with future volatility (finance: $r = -0.32$);
  \item elevated cosmos-orbit rates (EEG seizure: $\Delta R^2 = +0.19$, 15 patients);
  \item reduced orbit return rates (audio: partial $r = +0.75$).
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{QA as a Measurement Framework}

The central claim of this paper is not that QA predicts specific outcomes
(return direction, seizure timing, signal identity). It is that
\emph{deviation from QA-native transition dynamics carries forward-looking
structural information about system stability}. QA provides a measurement
layer: a discrete algebraic framework into which continuous observables can
be ingested and structurally organized.

This positions QA alongside other structural frameworks:
\begin{itemize}
  \item In physics, symmetries describe system structure, not individual events.
  \item In control theory, state classification precedes control design.
  \item In QA, orbit coherence measures structural health before
    domain-specific prediction.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The microstate-to-QA mapping is designed, not learned. The mapping
    must ensure orbit diversity, which constrains the design space.
  \item The finance result is strongest at $K=5$--$6$ clusters and degrades
    at $K=4$. The sensitivity to cluster count suggests the signal is
    structured but not universal across all configurations.
  \item Seismology does not reach significance ($p \approx 0.12$), and the
    climate teleconnection signal is structural (orbit discrimination) but
    not predictive (QCI does not predict future dispersion).
  \item The EEG signal resides in the microstate \emph{distribution}, not
    temporal dynamics (Section~\ref{sec:surrogates}). This limits
    interpretation as a dynamical coherence measure.
  \item The multi-layer observer architecture (Section~\ref{sec:separation})
    is validated for finance but not yet tested cross-domain.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Future Work}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Test multi-layer observer (local + global + fusion) cross-domain (EEG, audio, ERA5).
  \item Seismology: expanded testing (20 years, 8 channels, 16 parameter
    configurations) did not reach significance; alternative observer designs
    (e.g., spatial clustering of epicenters) may be needed.
  \item Develop the EEG distributional finding: why does microstate \emph{distribution}
    but not \emph{temporal order} drive orbit discrimination?
  \item Investigate theoretical connections to phase-conjugate and pump-wave
    frameworks in nonlinear physics (the opposite-sign structure of local
    and global QCI bears resemblance to pump-conjugate dynamics).
  \item Pre-registered cross-domain invariance test: given fixed T-operator and
    parameters, does a domain-classification law ($\Phi(D)$) correctly predict
    the QCI sign on new domains without retuning?
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}
% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We have shown that the QA Fibonacci-shift operator, applied to topographically
clustered multi-channel signals, produces a coherence index with genuine
predictive power for future system instability. The result is hardened
across out-of-sample testing, robustness grids, permutation tests,
partial correlation controls, and --- critically --- process-level
surrogate validation with corrected null design. Five of seven domains
(finance, ERA5 atmosphere, audio, EEG, cardiac) survive this stringent
test, confirming the architecture is domain-general. The cardiac domain
was pre-registered as disorder-stress before any results were computed,
providing the first successful blind $\Phi(D)$ classification.

The deeper insight is foundational: continuous observables are pushforwards
of path measures on QA reachability graphs under observer projection.
QA does not approximate observables --- it defines the \emph{admissible
manifold} from which they are generated. The T-operator coherence measures
how closely a system's observed dynamics conform to the algebraic constraints
of this manifold. When conformity breaks down, the system is departing from
its own structural law --- and instability follows.

Unlike Koopman operator methods~\cite{koopman1931}, which lift nonlinear
dynamics to an infinite-dimensional function space requiring truncation
and approximation,
QA operates on a finite, algebraically closed state graph, enabling exact
reachability and obstruction analysis. This positions QA not as a feature
extractor but as a \emph{state-space lens}: a discrete algebraic framework
that constrains the problem \emph{before} learning happens.
