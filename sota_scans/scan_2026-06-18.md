### 1. Tao/SAIR Third Competition: Inverse Galois Challenge — Degree 24
**Rating: HIGH** (June 16, 2026; new in window)
**Organizers:** John Jones, Jen Paulhus, David Roe, Andrew Sutherland, Terence Tao (with LMFDB)
**URL:** https://terrytao.wordpress.com/2026/06/16/third-sair-competition-inverse-galois-challenge/

**Summary:** The third SAIR competition launches an AI-assisted brute-force attack on the inverse Galois problem at degree 24 — determining which finite groups can be realized as Galois groups of degree-24 extensions of Q. Targets approximately 25,000 transitive subgroups of S_24. Stage 1 (closing August 15, 2026): AI tools brute-force as many of the 25,000 groups as possible. Stage 2: more mathematically sophisticated attack on resistant cases. Organized in collaboration with LMFDB (L-functions and Modular Forms Database).

**QA Mapping Potential:** Direct structural overlap at three levels:

1. **Degree 24 = QA's primary modulus.** The challenge's central object is degree-24 extensions of Q, whose Galois groups act on a 24-element set — exactly Z/24Z, QA's Cosmos orbit state space. The 25,000 transitive subgroups of S_24 include the cyclic group Z/24Z itself (generated by the T-operator). QA's T-operator on the Cosmos orbit generates a specific cyclic subgroup of S_24; this challenge builds the computational database of all such groups — including QA's.

2. **LMFDB data = Langlands + Frobenius data.** The LMFDB stores L-functions, modular forms, and number field data — the same Langlands material formalized in the sprint [403]–[412]. The Frobenius-at-primes data classifying prime splitting in Q(sqrt(5))/Q (certs [415]–[418]) is standard LMFDB format. This challenge will generate new degree-24 Galois group data in LMFDB that QA's Pisano/Frobenius certs can cross-reference directly.

3. **AI+Lean toolkit extension.** SAIR Stage 2 (scan #11) established the Lean 4 formalization pathway for modular arithmetic over Z/mZ. That toolkit now extends to degree-24 Galois group identification.

**Why HIGH (not MEDIUM-HIGH):** The challenge is *realizing* degree-24 Galois groups as splitting fields of polynomials over Q — an existence problem. QA's T-operator already *is* a specific cyclic group action on 24 elements; the challenge builds the database that classifies all such actions. The connection is "QA's Cosmos orbit is a degree-24 Galois group; this challenge builds the computational atlas." The atlas unlocks the cross-reference, not a structural theorem about QA's orbit.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_inverse_galois_degree24_cert_v1` (NEW — HIGH priority). Certifies: (1) QA's T-operator on the 24-element Cosmos orbit generates the cyclic transitive subgroup Z/24Z <= S_24 — a valid degree-24 cyclic Galois group realization; (2) LMFDB degree-24 cyclic number fields have decomposition groups at unramified primes that match QA's prime-splitting trichotomy (cert [415]). ### 2. arXiv:2606.15506 — Dual Affine Spiral Orbits on Z^2 Generated by Paired Unit Squares
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped scan #12; June 13, 2026)
**Author:** Chuck Wrathall (Independent Researcher)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15506

**Summary:** Geometric iteration on the integer lattice Z^2: paired unit squares sharing a corner generate two interlocking affine spiral orbits. Positive orbit {P_n} starts at (0,0); negative orbit {N_n} starts at (-1,2). Both satisfy linear recurrences driven by multiplication by the Gaussian integer 1+i. Invariant: P_n + N_n = (-1,2) for all n. Extending backwards via inverse maps produces an IFS attractor similar to the Twindragon fractal. Sequence appears as OEIS A396151.

**QA Mapping Potential:** (1) Dual orbit structure with preserved invariant (P_n + N_n = const) parallels QA's Cosmos/Satellite orbit pairing with additive structure preserved by the T-operator. (2) Discrete-to-fractal bridge via inverse IFS mirrors QA's Theorem NT / T2 structure: discrete orbit iteration (QA layer) → continuous fractal limit (observer layer).

**Why LOW-MEDIUM:** The orbit is infinite and spiral, not finite and periodic. Group is Z[i] (Gaussian integers), not Z/mZ. No modular quotient taken.

**Cert scaffolding:** None standalone. Fourth backbone reference for `qa_orbit_stratification_cert_v1` [261] human tract — Twindragon orbit duality as a concrete dual-orbit-with-fractal-limit toy model over Z^2.

### 3. arXiv:2510.23298 — Galois Groups of Apery-like Series Modulo Primes
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (October 2025; mega-slip — all 13 scans)
**Authors:** Xavier Caruso, Florian Fürnsinn, Daniel Vargas-Montoya, Wadim Zudilin
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23298

**Summary:** Computes Galois groups of mod-p reductions of Apery numbers, Domb numbers, and Almkvist-Zudilin numbers. Key result: these Galois groups are governed by **congruence conditions on p**, including explicit mod-24 conditions (p ≡ 1, 5 mod 24 and p ≡ 13, 17 mod 24). Published in Bulletin of the Australian Mathematical Society.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The mod-24 prime congruence structure is the same arithmetic layer as QA's prime splitting in Q(sqrt(5))/Q (cert [415]) and the [414]–[418] cert cluster's mod-24 arithmetic. Independent confirmation that mod-24 is the natural classification modulus for prime arithmetic relevant to QA.

**Why LOW-MEDIUM:** The Apery-like series are continuous hypergeometric series; the mod-p reduction is a specialization to characteristic p, not a natural Z/mZ dynamics. Arithmetic coincidence rather than structural isomorphism.

**Cert scaffolding:** None standalone. Cross-reference annotation for certs [415]–[418] human tracts.
