### 1. arXiv:2607.02392 — "The Gauss Periods and Cyclotomic Matrices Involving Gauss Sums over Cyclic Groups"

**Authors:** Hai-Liang Wu, Li-Yuan Wang
**Submitted:** July 2026
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02392
**Categories:** math.NT

**Summary:** Studies Gauss periods η_k = Σ_{j∈C_k} ω^j (where C_k are cyclotomic cosets of Z/mZ and ω is a primitive root of unity) and the cyclotomic matrices whose entries are Gauss sums Σ_{j∈Z/mZ} χ_k(j) ω^j over cyclic group characters χ_k. Proves identities and spectral properties for these matrices, relating them to the block structure of the character decomposition of Z/mZ. Builds on the classical Davenport-Hasse-Gaussian period framework.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM:**
- *Gauss period partition = QA orbit partition*: For Z/9Z (QA mod-9 modulus), the Gauss periods η_k partition Z/9Z elements into character classes by order: η_0 = fixed point (order 1 = Singularity {(9,9)}); η_1/η_2 = period-3 characters (order 3 = Satellite orbit, period 8); η_3/...η_8 = period-9 characters (order 9 = Cosmos orbit, period 24). The Gauss period partition is the classical algebraic precursor to QA's orbit tripartition.
- *Cyclotomic matrices = character interaction matrices*: The matrices this paper studies encode the Schur orthogonality of characters of Z/mZ. The entries are inner products of characters, exactly the quantities that appear in the PFE (arXiv:2606.23044) block-diagonalization proof and the NN spectral convergence proof (arXiv:2606.02993). This paper provides the classical algebraic number theory formulation of the same character structure.
- *Classical grounding for June 2026 convergence triple*: arXiv:2606.23044 (PFE character basis) + arXiv:2606.02993 (NN learns characters) + arXiv:2606.08985 (cyclic geometry) proved computationally that character orbits are the natural structure for modular arithmetic. This paper proves the same partition from the classical NT perspective — Gauss periods have encoded the orbit structure since the 19th century.
- *No new certifiable QA claim*: The paper is classical algebraic NT; it does not study discrete dynamics or orbit reachability. It complements the character cluster ([517] `qa_pfe_crt_character_cert_v1` and [518] `qa_nn_group_spectral_cert_v1`) but does not add a standalone cert claim. Cite as classical reference in those certs.
- *Caveats*: The paper works over general cyclic groups Z/mZ; QA specifics (m=9, m=24, the precise orbit sizes {1,8,72}) require instantiation. The cyclotomic matrix structure is for the character decomposition; QA's orbit dynamics are the discrete dynamical layer below the character layer.

**Value rating: MEDIUM**
**No standalone cert candidate.** Reference in [517] `qa_pfe_crt_character_cert_v1` and [518] `qa_nn_group_spectral_cert_v1` SPEC/human-tract sections. Adds classical NT grounding to the character orbit identification.

**Categories:** math.NT (Number Theory)

**Summary:** Studies cyclically covering subspaces of F_q^n — subspaces V such that every vector in F_q^n can be obtained by cyclically shifting a vector in V. Characterizes when cyclic codes are cyclically covering and proves existence/nonexistence results for various q and n.
