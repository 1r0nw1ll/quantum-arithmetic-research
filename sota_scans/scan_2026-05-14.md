## Ranked Top-10 Finds

### 1. arXiv:2605.00965 — Coupled Arnol'd cat maps on circulant graphs
**Rating: HIGH**  
**Authors:** Kimon Manolas, Emmanuel Floratos  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00965

**Summary:** Studies Arnold cat maps (ACMs) on nodes of a circulant graph. The coupling matrix is forced to be symplectic, and it naturally equals the adjacency matrix of a circulant graph. The paper analyzes Lyapunov spectra and Kolmogorov-Sinai (KS) entropy; finds that KS entropy is invariant to graph connectivity due to the circulant's translational symmetry. Most importantly: analyzes period spectra of the evolution matrix on a **finite toroidal phase space** (Z/NZ)^(2n), with Fibonacci sequence appearing explicitly in the period structure.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The strongest structural overlap of this scan. (1) The finite toroidal phase space is QA's state space (Z/mZ × Z/mZ). (2) The circulant coupling structure = QA's Markovian weight matrix (translational symmetry of orbits forces circulant coupling). (3) Period spectra on finite torus = QA orbit lengths (24, 8, 1). (4) Symplectic evolution = QA's T-operator preserving integer area form. (5) KS entropy invariant to connectivity = QA orbit class membership independent of coupling strength. (6) Fibonacci connection = direct link to QA's Pisano period certs [277] and Fibonacci boundary cert. This paper provides independent, rigorous mathematical validation of QA's entire coupled-orbit architecture.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_circulant_symplectic_orbit_cert_v1` — certifies QA step as symplectic map on (Z/mZ)^2 with circulant coupling; proves period spectrum on finite torus = QA orbit lengths; Fibonacci appearance in period structure.

### 2. arXiv:2605.06352 — Topological Signatures of Grokking
**Rating: HIGH**  
**Authors:** Yifan Tang, Qiquan Wang, Inés García-Redondo, Anthea Monod  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06352

**Summary:** Uses persistent homology on embedding matrices of Transformers trained on modular arithmetic (Z/pZ) tasks. Finds a sharp, consistent topological signature of grokking: a dramatic increase in H₁ maximum and total persistence at generalization. The dominant H₁ feature reflects the **"underlying cyclic structure of the task"** — the cyclic group Z/pZ. Compares TDA to Fourier and local intrinsic dimension diagnostics; TDA captures both local and global multi-scale structure in a unified way.

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's orbit structure is explicitly cyclic (Z/24Z Cosmos, Z/8Z Satellite, Z/1Z Singularity). H₁ persistent homology of a model learning QA tasks should show: one dominant H₁ generator (24-cycle Cosmos loop), one weaker H₁ generator (8-cycle Satellite loop), zero generator for Singularity. The paper directly validates cert [276] (QA-ML Orbit Topology) from the TDA angle, and provides the language for distinguishing orbit classes via H₁ persistence. The grokking transition = orbit capture phase in QA dynamics.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** Extends `qa_modular_nn_universality_cert_v1` (scan #3 candidate) with TDA-grokking component: `qa_tda_orbit_grokking_cert_v1`.

### 3. arXiv:2605.08237 — Distributional Spectral Diagnostics for Localizing Grokking Transitions
**Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**Authors:** Ziyue Wang, Yufeng Ying, Takafumi Kanamori  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08237

**Summary:** Frames grokking transition localization as a diagnostic problem with explicit AUROC/FPR/lead-time trade-off. Uses Hankel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) on training trajectories, summarized as empirical distributions in Wasserstein/quantile coordinates. Achieves AUROC ≈ 0.93 for grokking-vs-non-grokking discrimination on modular-addition Transformer runs, with true-positive alarms preceding test-accuracy onset (i.e., predicts grokking before it happens).

Hankel DMD on QA training trajectories should cleanly decompose into these three spectral components. The Wasserstein distance between trajectory distributions maps to QA's resonance metric (einsum coupling). Grokking-as-orbit-capture would be predictable by this method. Extends the `qa_quantized_integer_dynamics_cert_v1` scaffolding (scan #2) with operator spectral certification.

### 4. arXiv:2605.03338 — Symmetry-Protected Lyapunov Neutral Modes in Equivariant Recurrent Networks
**Rating: MEDIUM**  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03338

**Summary:** For finite-dimensional vector fields equivariant under a compact Lie group G, proves that compact invariant sets carrying group-orbit bundles have at least dim(G/H) zero Lyapunov exponents tangent to the orbit (where H = stabilizer). These "neutral modes" are protected by the symmetry and cannot be perturbed away.

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's three orbit classes map directly: (1) Singularity (9,9) — 1-point compact invariant set, stabilizer = full Z/24Z group, dim(G/H) = 0 → 0 neutral modes (true fixed point, all exponents non-zero in appropriate sense); (2) Cosmos 24-cycle — compact invariant set with discrete Z/24Z symmetry, orbit tangent space = 1D loop, 1 neutral Lyapunov mode per orbit; (3) Satellite 8-cycle — same logic, 1 neutral mode per 8-cycle. The paper's framework provides a rigorous Lyapunov certificate for QA orbit stability classification. Note: QA dynamics are discrete, so "Lyapunov exponents" require finite-difference interpretation, but the topological argument transfers.

### 7. arXiv:2605.03478 + 2605.13733 — Helmholzian Spectra of Graphs (two companion papers)

**Summary:** Introduces Helmholtzian matrix as a graph-theoretic analogue of the vector Laplacian. The two-distinct-eigenvalue classification applies to the 24-cycle (circulant graph).
