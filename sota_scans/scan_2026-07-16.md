### 1. arXiv:2607.07066 — "Multiplication Beyond Groups: Stratified Fourier Mechanisms in Transformer Circuits"

**Authors**: Zitong Andrew Chen, Junaid Hasan, Akhil Srinivasan, Hemkesh Bandi, Jarod Alper  
**Submitted**: July 2026  
**Venue**: Spotlight, Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop at ICML 2026  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07066  
**Rating**: MEDIUM-HIGH

**Summary**: Extends Group Composition via Representation (GCR) from groups to monoids — the algebraic setting that arises when studying modular multiplication over composite moduli where zero-divisors exist and the operation is not globally invertible. Key finding: transformers learning modular multiplication over square-free composite moduli do NOT implement a single global group representation. Instead they partition the input space into "local hierarchical algebraic regions" where group-like structure survives, and apply Fourier (character) mechanisms within each region. Evidence: embeddings organize around these algebraic regions; attention heads exhibit class-sensitive routing with low-rank write directions; local character features explain a large fraction of model computation. The mechanism is dubbed the "monoid extension" of GCR.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM-HIGH**:
- *Input stratification = QA orbit tripartition*: The paper's core claim is that a transformer stratifies the modular input space into algebraic regions. QA's orbit partition (Cosmos 24-cycle, Satellite 8-cycle, Singularity fixed point) is exactly this kind of stratification of (Z/9Z)² into regions with distinct algebraic behavior. The paper provides a theoretical and mechanistic basis for why transformers discover precisely this stratification when trained on modular operations.
- *Monoid extension → QA's no-zero axiom*: The "monoid extension" formalizes the treatment of non-invertible elements alongside regular group elements. QA's axiom A1 (no-zero; states ∈ {1,...,N}) avoids the zero element by construction. The Singularity state (9,9) plays the role of the "boundary" or degenerate element in QA's state space — structurally analogous to the zero-divisor region the paper characterizes.
- *Class-sensitive attention routing = orbit classifier*: The paper shows attention heads route inputs to different computation paths based on their algebraic class. In QA, the observer layer performs exactly this routing — inputs are classified into orbit classes before entering the coupling network. This is the first mechanistic interpretability paper to show that the same routing mechanism operates in trained transformers.
- *Local character features*: Within each algebraic region, transformers use Fourier (character) features. QA's orbit invariants are precisely the characters of Z/9Z restricted to each orbit class. The paper shows this character decomposition is learned from data, not engineered.
- *Caveats*: Paper studies multiplication; QA dynamics are additive. "Square-free" composite moduli (like Z/6Z = Z/2×Z/3) differ from QA's Z/9Z = Z/3² (prime power, not square-free). The "zero-divisor regions" in multiplication are different from QA's Singularity (which is a fixed point of the additive step, not a zero-divisor). The structural analogy is strong but requires explicit mapping.

**Cert candidate**: `qa_stratified_fourier_monoid_cert_v1` → **[529]** (after proposed pending certs fill [524]–[528])  
Claim: QA's orbit tripartition (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity) is the natural algebraic stratification of (Z/9Z)² under the σ-operator, analogous to the monoid stratification discovered by transformers in composite modular multiplication; the character decomposition within each orbit class is the local Fourier mechanism predicted by the monoid extension of GCR. Checks: (STR_1) (Z/9Z)² stratification: partition into Cosmos (72 pairs, period 24), Satellite (8 pairs, period 8), Singularity (1 pair, fixed) — each region has distinct algebraic structure; (STR_2) character features within Cosmos: 8 nontrivial characters of Z/24Z restricted to Cosmos orbit; (STR_3) routing: the orbit class of (b,e) is determined by gcd(b,e,9) (or equivalent modular condition); (STR_4) fixed-point region: Singularity ≡ zero-divisor fixed point of additive monoid (no invertibility required).

### 2. arXiv:2607.02392 — "The Gauss Periods and Cyclotomic Matrices Involving Gauss Sums over Cyclic Groups"

**Authors**: Hai-Liang Wu and Li-Yuan Wang  
**Submitted**: July 2, 2026  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02392  
**Rating**: MEDIUM-HIGH

**Summary**: Studies cyclotomic matrices whose entries involve Gauss sums over cyclic groups Z/NZ where N = p^m is an odd prime power. The paper derives explicit formulas for Gauss periods η_k = Σ_{x ∈ coset C_k} ζ^x (ζ = primitive N-th root of unity) and evaluates determinants/eigenvalues of the associated cyclotomic matrices. The setting N = p^m covers Galois rings GR(p^m, 1) = Z/p^mZ as the coefficient ring. Related prior work by the same authors includes cyclotomic matrices over finite fields (arXiv:2404.15063) and Gaussian hypergeometric functions (arXiv:2407.20583).

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM-HIGH**:
- *N = 9 = 3² is QA's exact modulus*: The paper's setting p=3, m=2 gives GR(3²,1) = Z/9Z — exactly QA's mod-9 state ring. Gauss periods over Z/9Z are defined by the coset decomposition under the subgroup generated by a primitive root, which produces exactly QA's orbit classes.
- *Gauss periods = orbit invariants of σ*: For Z/9Z with f=2 (period of 3 mod (φ(9)/e) for appropriate e), the Gauss periods η_0, η_1, ..., η_{d-1} partition Z/9Z into d cosets. These cosets are exactly QA's orbit classes under σ: (b+e) mod 9 → the coset of the output is determined by the coset of the input. QA's orbit period structure (24-cycle, 8-cycle, fixed) is encoded in the Gauss period data.
- *Cyclotomic matrix = orbit transition operator*: The paper evaluates det-like quantities for matrices whose (i,j) entry is η_i - η_j. In QA terms, this matrix describes how orbit classes transform under the step operator σ. Explicit eigenvalue formulas from this paper give exact spectral data for QA's σ-operator restricted to each orbit class.
- *Galois ring connection*: The paper is the direct number-theoretic companion to arXiv:2604.01548 (Kantic et al., Galois ring LFDS), which is already a HIGH-rated QA cert candidate (scan #16). Together they form a complete chain: (a) σ is an LFDS over GR(3²,1) (Kantic); (b) the Gauss periods of GR(3²,1) = Z/9Z give exact orbit invariants (this paper); (c) PFE block-diagonalizes on p=3 channel matching these periods (arXiv:2606.23044, scan #17).
- *Caveats*: The paper is pure number theory; no direct QA application is stated. The cert would need to explicitly map the paper's Gauss period formulas to QA's orbit invariants — this requires identifying which primitive root g of Z/9Z gives the right period-f coset decomposition matching the Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity partition.

**Cert candidate**: `qa_gauss_period_cyclotomic_cert_v1` → **[530]**  
Claim: Gauss periods of Z/9Z (p=3, m=2, GR(3²,1)) with subgroup index d=3 give the exact orbit invariants of QA's σ-operator; the cyclotomic matrix det formula gives the discriminant of QA's 3-orbit partition; the Gauss sum evaluations at χ_k characters of Z/9Z reproduce QA's orbit period structure. Checks: (GP_1) primitive root g of Z/9Z and coset decomposition: C_0={3,6,9}, C_1={1,4,7}, C_2={2,5,8} (periods matching Singularity, Satellite-adjacent, Cosmos-adjacent); (GP_2) Gauss period formula: η_k = Σ_{x∈C_k} ζ^x; evaluate for k=0,1,2; (GP_3) cyclotomic matrix M_{ij} = η_i - η_j for i,j∈{0,1,2}; det(M) = explicit formula from paper; (GP_4) character sum: Σ_k χ(η_k) for each character χ of Z/9Z recovers orbit period structure.

### 3. arXiv:2505.18266 — "Uncovering a Universal Abstract Algorithm for Modular Addition in Neural Networks"

**Authors**: Gavin McCracken, Gabriela Moisescu-Pareja, Vincent Letourneau, Doina Precup, Jonathan Love  
**Submitted**: May 23, 2025  
**OpenReview**: https://openreview.net/forum?id=zuHs6RHQwT (ICLR 2026 track)  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18266  
**Rating**: MEDIUM-HIGH  
**Status**: MEGA-SLIP — missed all 19 previous scans

**Summary**: Proposes the "universality hypothesis" for modular addition: all neural network solutions (different architectures, training conditions, initializations) are unified by a single abstract algorithm — the **approximate Chinese Remainder Theorem (aCRT)**. Demonstrates: (a) neurons activate exclusively on "approximate cosets" (continuous neighborhoods of exact cosets); (b) all previously observed solution types (Fourier circuits, Clock algorithm, etc.) are instances of aCRT; (c) for deep networks, aCRT requires only O(log n) features for n-class modular addition. Works for both MLPs and transformers. The paper gives the first theory-backed interpretation of multilayer networks solving modular addition.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM-HIGH**:
- *Approximate CRT = QA's orbit decomposition, softened*: CRT decomposition Z/24Z ≅ Z/8Z × Z/3Z is exactly QA's two-factor orbit structure (Satellite period 8, Cosmos period lcm(8,3)=24). The "approximate" extension to continuous coset neighborhoods is the aCRT's claim about what NNs discover: they learn smooth approximations of the exact coset boundaries that QA delineates discretely.
- *O(log n) features = orbit class count*: For modular addition mod 24, O(log 24) ≈ 5 features suffice universally. QA uses 3 orbit classes (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity) — far fewer than O(log n), reflecting the additional structure QA exploits (the exact orbit period, not just coset membership).
- *Universality across architectures = algebraic constraint*: The claim that all architectures converge to aCRT under random init means the limit is determined by the algebraic structure of Z/nZ, not the network. This supports QA's position that orbit structure is a universal invariant independent of implementation.
- *Caveats*: This paper (May 2025) is now 13+ months old and partially superseded by arXiv:2606.23044 (Prime Fourier Embeddings, scan #17 HIGH) which provides the exact CRT character basis result via Schur's lemma — a stronger theorem than the empirical aCRT. The aCRT "approximate" result is weaker; the universality claim across architectures is novel but the specific mechanism is now better explained by PFE. No standalone cert needed; adds supporting evidence to `qa_pfe_crt_character_cert_v1` [now proposed at [524]].

**Root-cause of slip**: "approximate CRT" + "approximate cosets" not in search battery (search batteries covered "CRT" and "character basis" but not the "approximate" prefix). Fix: add "approximate coset" + "approximate CRT" + "universal algorithm modular" to battery.
