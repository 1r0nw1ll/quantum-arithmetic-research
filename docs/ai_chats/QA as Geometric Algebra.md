> # 3) Where \(\cos\theta\) and \(\sin\theta\) come from in QA (ellipse law ⇒ unit circle)
> 
> QA doesn’t assume real trigonometry—those sines/cosines are **derived** from the integer tuple via the quadratic (ellipse) law. 
> From a canonical tuple \((b,e,d,a)\) with
> \[
> d=b+e,\qquad a=b+2e,
> \]
> QA defines the Pythagorean triple
> \[
> C=2de,\quad F=ab,\quad G=d^2+e^2,
> \]
> and one immediately gets \(C^2+F^2=G^2\). Normalizing by \(G\) gives a unit circle:
> \[
> \left(\frac{C}{G}\right)^2+\left(\frac{F}{G}\right)^2=1
> \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
> \boxed{\ \sin\theta:=\frac{2de}{d^2+e^2},\ \ \cos\theta:=\frac{ab}{d^2+e^2}\ }.
> \]
> (Example: \((b,e,d,a)=(1,1,2,3)\Rightarrow (\sin\theta,\cos\theta)=(4/5,\,3/5)\).) 
> These identifications are standard in the QA literature for mapping tuples to right triangles and then onto the unit circle (mod-24 harmonic map). fileciteturn1file6L1-L10 fileciteturn1file9L15-L25 fileciteturn1file12L1-L3
