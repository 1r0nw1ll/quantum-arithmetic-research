### 1. arXiv:2607.19172 — "On the autotopism groups and the equivalence of finite cyclic semifields"

**Authors**: Paolo Santonastaso, Yue Zhou
**Submitted**: July 21, 2026
**Categories**: math.CO, math.RA (implied)
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19172
**Rating**: LOW

**Summary**: Provides a complete classification of finite cyclic semifields up to isotopy and determines their autotopism groups in full, closing open cases left by Dempwolff (2011) since Sandler's foundational 1962 construction. A cyclic semifield is a finite presemifield defined by a cyclic vector space automorphism over a finite field; its "autotopism group" is the group of symmetries of the multiplication table. The resolution requires careful case analysis based on the degree of the field extension relative to the polynomial defining the semifield multiplication.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW**:
- *"Cyclic semifield" ≠ cyclic group orbit*: In this context "cyclic" refers to the algebraic construction method — the multiplication map is defined via a cyclic vector space automorphism (a specific linear map). This is not about Z/nZ dynamics or orbit classification under iteration. Cyclic semifields are related to projective planes and spread sets in finite geometry.
- *Autotopism group ≠ QA orbit symmetry*: QA's three orbit classes (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity) arise from the dynamics of σ on Z/mZ. The autotopism group of a semifield classifies when two semifields are algebraically equivalent — a purely structural question, not a dynamical one.
- *Presemifields over finite fields*: The ambient object (a vector space over GF(p^k)) is not the same as QA's ambient ring Z/mZ. Semifields by definition have no zero divisors; Z/9Z has zero divisors and that's essential to QA's structure.
- **Verdict**: LOW. Resolves a decades-old open problem in finite geometry, but no genuine structural overlap with QA.

### 2. arXiv:2607.13442 — "Periodicities in the Riordan arrays of polynomials over finite fields"

**Authors**: (not retrieved — arXiv HTTP 403)
**Submitted**: July 15, 2026
**Categories**: math.CO, math.NT
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13442
**Rating**: LOW-MEDIUM

**Summary**: Studies 2-D and 3-D Riordan arrays over a finite field GF(q). Main results: (1) columns of the 2-D Riordan array are eventually periodic sequences; a circulant matrix generated by the polynomial coefficients p_3(t) governs the periodicity as the column index grows; (2) preperiodic column partial sums are themselves periodic, with a zero-sum subfamily identified; (3) the layers of the 3-D array contain periodic orbits related to each other via powers of a circulant matrix generated by p_4(t). The parent work is arXiv:2308.02656 which developed the circulant-matrix periodicity machinery for the real/integer case.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW-MEDIUM**:
- *Circulant matrix + periodic orbits in finite field*: The core technical tool (circulant matrix generates orbit structure for successive "layers" of the array) is genuinely parallel to QA's step operator structure: σ on Z/mZ is a cyclic shift operator whose orbit structure on pairs (b,e) is also governed by a companion matrix. The "layers are periodic orbits related via powers of a circulant" sentence maps almost directly onto "Cosmos/Satellite orbits are periodic cycles, shifted relatives under σ^k applications."
- *Riordan arrays are combinatorial, not dynamical*: Riordan arrays (infinite lower-triangular matrices defined by a pair of generating functions) are primarily tools for combinatorial identities (Pascal's triangle, Catalan, Motzkin, etc.). The periodicity over GF(q) is a modular reduction of these arrays, not a discrete dynamical system on a state space. The circulant matrix structure emerges from the polynomial defining the Riordan pair — it is algebraic, not dynamical.
- *No discrete-to-continuous bridge*: Riordan array periodicities don't obviously connect to a continuous observer layer. QA's architecture requires a T2 Firewall crossing; Riordan arrays have no such structure.
- *Supporting evidence for [527]*: Together with arXiv:2607.12761 (scan #21, cyclic projective orbits, MDS codes over F_q), this paper adds another example where circulant/cyclic operator orbit classification appears naturally over finite algebraic structures. Both confirm that the tripartite/layered orbit structure is a general phenomenon, but the specific setting (GF(q) matrices) is simpler than QA's prime-power ring Z/9Z. Add to [527] evidence file, not a standalone cert.
- **Verdict**: LOW-MEDIUM. Contributes weak supporting evidence to `qa_linear_fd_galois_ring_cert_v1` [527]. The circulant matrix + layered periodic orbit structure is a methodological parallel to QA's step operator orbit theory. No standalone cert.

### 4. arXiv:2606.02621 — "A Fibonacci theorem for Collatz trajectories via modular graph structure" (MEGA-SLIP)

**Author**: Manuel-Alejandro Reyes Jiménez
**Submitted**: May 28, 2026
**Categories**: math.NT
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02621
**Paper size**: 24 pages, 1 figure
**Rating**: MEDIUM
**Status**: MEGA-SLIP — missed scans #13 through #21 (8+ consecutive scans since May 28)

**Summary**: For each m ≥ 1, exactly F(m+1) odd integers in {1,...,2^m} have the property that their Collatz orbit avoids the residue class 4 (mod 6) during steps 2,...,m, where F(m+1) is the (m+1)-th Fibonacci number. The proportion decays at rate (φ/2)^m where φ = (1+√5)/2. The proof uses the directed graph G of Collatz transitions modulo 6; the unique absorbing strongly connected component G' = G[{1,2,4,5}]; removing vertex 4 from G' yields a subgraph with spectral radius φ, against ρ(G') = 2, and the Fibonacci count follows from this spectral gap.

**QA Mapping Assessment — MEDIUM**:
- *Modular Collatz directed graph → absorbing SCC*: The construction (define a directed graph of transitions under the Collatz map modulo M; identify the absorbing SCC; classify orbits by which SCC they converge to) is methodologically identical to QA's orbit stratification. QA defines a directed graph of σ-transitions on Z/mZ × Z/mZ and identifies three absorbing components: the 24-cycle Cosmos, the 8-cycle Satellite, the 1-point Singularity. The paper's G → G' → (G' minus vertex 4) hierarchy is a structural parallel to QA's Z/9Z → Cosmos → (orbit without Singularity) progression.
- *Fibonacci count → Pisano period connection*: The Fibonacci numbers appearing as orbit counts in the modular Collatz graph are connected to QA's Fibonacci/Pisano cert [441] (qa_witt_tower_fibonacci_pisano_cert) and the broader Fibonacci/Pisano thread. The spectral radius φ for the Collatz mod-6 subgraph is the golden ratio — QA's Pell-Sturmian cert [295] and Fibonacci Pisano cert [441] already explore irrational/Fibonacci connections.
- *Specific to Collatz dynamics (3n+1)*: The Fibonacci count emerges from the Collatz-specific ratio 3/2 (approximately). On a general modular transition graph the spectral radius would be different. So the Fibonacci appearance is Collatz-specific, not a universal property of modular orbit graphs.
- *Modulus 6 vs. QA moduli (9, 24)*: The mod-6 structure is natural for Collatz (3n+1 interacts with 2 and 3, the prime factors of 6). QA uses mod-9 and mod-24 for different reasons. The fact that 6 | 24 means there's a quotient map from Z/24Z to Z/6Z, but using it to bridge Collatz → QA would be artificial.
- *Potential cert note*: This paper does not warrant a standalone cert family — the Fibonacci connection is already in [441], and the absorbing SCC methodology is implicit in [261] (orbit stratification). However, it could serve as a cross-reference example in [441] showing the Fibonacci spectral gap in a modular orbit graph. Worth flagging in [441] evidence file.
- *Root cause of mega-slip*: Search battery lacked "Fibonacci Collatz modular" until scan #21 (added as recommendation but not yet run). Fix confirmed: term now active.
- **Verdict**: MEDIUM. Genuine structural overlap (modular directed graph, absorbing SCC decomposition, Fibonacci spectral count) with QA's orbit stratification methodology. Not a cert candidate — adds to [441] and [261] cross-reference evidence. MEGA-SLIP confirmed.

### 5. arXiv:2603.25753 — "A Structural Reduction of the Collatz Conjecture to One-Bit Orbit Mixing" (MEGA-SLIP)

**Author**: Edward Y. Chang (Stanford University)
**Submitted**: March 24, 2026
**Categories**: math.NT, math.DS
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25753
**Rating**: LOW-MEDIUM
**Status**: MEGA-SLIP — missed scans #~10 through #21 (10+ scans since March 24)

**Summary**: Reduces the Collatz conjecture to a fixed-modulus, one-bit orbit-mixing problem using the compressed odd-to-odd Collatz map. Proves exact low-depth decomposition formulas at depths K=3,4,5. The Map Balance Theorem proves that among burst residues modulo 2^K, the counts mapping to gap starts ≡ 3 (mod 8) versus ≡ 7 (mod 8) differ by exactly 1 for every K ≥ 5.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW-MEDIUM**:
- *Mod-8 binary orbit split ↔ QA's {Cosmos, Satellite}*: The Map Balance Theorem establishes a binary partition of Collatz burst residues into two classes (≡3 vs ≡7 mod 8) that are nearly balanced. QA's orbit structure also has a binary split: pairs with Satellite period (8-cycle, 8 pairs) vs. Cosmos period (24-cycle, 72 pairs). The periods are 8 and 24, and 8 | 24, so mod-8 is the natural orbit discriminant in both cases. However:
  - In Collatz: the ≡3/≡7 split is about burst starting residues in the compressed Collatz map — a classification of input integers, not cyclic orbit classes.
  - In QA: the Satellite/Cosmos split is about cyclic orbit classes in Z/24Z × Z/24Z under σ.
  - The concepts of "orbit" differ: Collatz orbits are non-periodic trajectories of natural numbers; QA orbits are periodic cycles of (b,e) pairs.
- *One-bit mixing ≠ QA coupling*: The "one-bit orbit mixing" reduction means Collatz reduces to showing that a 1-bit summary of burst residues mixes uniformly. QA's coupling is a matrix update from tuple resonance — different dynamics.
- *Mega-slip severity*: This paper has been available since March 2026 (through 10+ scans). Root cause: "Collatz structural reduction" + "orbit mixing" not in battery. Partially fixed in scan #21 recommendations.
- **Verdict**: LOW-MEDIUM. Superficial structural analogy (mod-8 binary orbit split). Interesting as context for QA's Satellite-period 8 cert, but the "orbit" concept is different. No cert candidate. Note in orbit stratification evidence.
