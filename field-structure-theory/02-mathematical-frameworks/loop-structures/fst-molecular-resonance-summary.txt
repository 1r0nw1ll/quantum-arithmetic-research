2. Abstract
This study develops a modular harmonic framework for modeling molecular vibrational behavior using Quantum Arithmetic (QA) and Field Structure Theory (FST). By scaling nested Planck-based loop structures up to the atomic and molecular level, we establish residue-based modular classifiers (mod-144 and mod-256) to predict chemical resonance and stability. The approach accurately maps IR vibrational frequencies of key molecules (e.g., CH₄, H₂O) to modular residues, yielding a direct correspondence between QA-derived residues and spectroscopic classes. The results provide a new pathway to unify quantum field models with real-world molecular behavior, potentially informing spectroscopy, quantum simulation, and material design.

3. Introduction
The intersection of Quantum Arithmetic and Field Structure Theory presents a unique opportunity to model matter not as particles in spacetime, but as harmonic loop structures nested within a modular numerical substrate. FST proposes that stable particles result from non-intersecting loops, while QA offers a computational language to model these loops with integer-based modular arithmetic.
In this research, we explore how molecular behavior, particularly vibrational resonance as seen in infrared (IR) spectroscopy, can be derived from the modular residue behavior of nested Planck-scale loop structures. Our guiding hypothesis is that molecular stability and vibrational class correspond to predictable modular closures. We focus on mod-144 (vibrational harmonic closure) and mod-256 (binary electronic closure) to categorize resonance behavior.

9. Limitations & Refinements
    • The linear mapping from mod-144 to 4000 cm⁻¹ is a first approximation.
    • Broader molecule sets need testing.
    • Bond coupling and intermodular interference effects are not yet modeled.
    • Current model treats all vibrations equally; does not weight atomic mass.
