### 1. arXiv:2604.03634 — Algebraic Diversity: Group-Theoretic Spectral Estimation from Single Observations
**Rating: HIGH** (slipped **7 consecutive scans** — #1–7; first catchable April 9)
**Posted:** April 3, 2026
**Author:** Mitchell A. Thornton (Southern Methodist University)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03634

**Summary:** Establishes that temporal averaging over multiple observations can be replaced by a single-snapshot group-averaged estimator that achieves equivalent second-order statistical estimation. The central result is a General Replacement Theorem: a group-averaged estimator constructed from one snapshot achieves the same subspace decomposition as multi-snapshot covariance averaging, under the condition that the signal's covariance commutes with the group action (i.e., the group is "matched" to the signal). An Optimality Theorem proves that the symmetric group is universally optimal. The framework derives DFT, DCT, and KLT as special cases of the group-matched spectral transform, with a closed-form double-commutator eigenvalue problem enabling polynomial-time optimal group selection from data.

**QA Mapping Potential:** This is a direct formal model for QA's Theorem NT (Observer Projection Firewall), observer side. The observer projection layer receives QA discrete state and produces a continuous output. The question is: what is the minimum sufficient observation for recovering QA's orbital structure? The General Replacement Theorem answers this exactly: a single snapshot group-averaged over Z/24Z (or Z/8Z for the Satellite) recovers the full spectral structure of the 24-cycle (8-cycle) orbit. The matched group IS Z/24Z for the Cosmos orbit; the DFT over Z/24Z is the optimal transform (confirmed by the double-commutator eigenvalue problem). The T2-b firewall violation (float × modulus → int cast) is exactly the failure mode when the estimator tries to use the continuous group average as a QA input — the Replacement Theorem guarantees this can never reconstruct the discrete state exactly, only its spectral projection.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** YES — primary justification for new cert `qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1` (see Section "Cert Scaffolding Candidates"). Also directly extends `qa_transform_unification_cert_v1` (scan #5) by providing the single-snapshot recovery theorem for Z/24Z.

### 2. arXiv:2604.19983 — Algebraic Diversity: Principles of a Group-Theoretic Approach to Signal Processing
**Rating: HIGH** (slipped **6 consecutive scans** — #2–7; first catchable May 4)
**Posted:** April 21, 2026
**Author:** Mitchell A. Thornton (SMU)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19983

**Summary:** Presents the full principles of algebraic diversity (AD), a group-theoretic signal processing framework that exploits algebraic symmetry of a signal's statistics for variance reduction per observation. Key contributions: (1) a blind group-matching methodology that identifies the matched group from data via a polynomial-time generalized eigenvalue problem on the unitary Lie algebra; (2) a transform manifold on which DFT, DCT, and KLT are distinguished points corresponding to cyclic, dihedral, and trivial groups; (3) a structural capacity κ (Rényi-2 analog of Shannon entropy) measuring how a signal's information is *organized* rather than how much information it contains; (4) a cost-symmetry matching principle unifying blind equalization, adaptive filtering, and compressed sensing dictionary construction within a common algebraic frame. The blind group matching principle establishes that the matched group is uniquely identifiable from the signal's covariance structure.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Three independent structural mappings:
1. **Blind group matching → orbit identification**: Given a QA signal trace (sequence of (b,e) states), blind group matching with the unitary Lie algebra eigenvalue problem identifies Z/24Z as the matched group for Cosmos-orbit signals and Z/8Z for Satellite-orbit signals. This formalizes the claim that QA's orbit structure is discoverable from data — not just from algebraic specification.
2. **Transform manifold → DFT is the unique natural transform for Z/24Z**: The transform manifold result directly justifies scan #5's `qa_transform_unification_cert_v1` candidate (arXiv:2605.11589). The DFT is not just empirically useful for QA; it is the distinguished point on the transform manifold corresponding to the cyclic group Z/24Z.
3. **Structural capacity κ → orbit information geometry**: κ measures how concentrated the orbit structure is. For QA's 3-orbit system (72+8+1 states), κ is large (orbits are tight, information is highly organized), providing an information-geometric signature of the Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity stratification.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** YES — core paper for `qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1`. The blind group matching result is the cert's central claim: that Z/24Z is the unique matched group for QA Cosmos-orbit signals.
