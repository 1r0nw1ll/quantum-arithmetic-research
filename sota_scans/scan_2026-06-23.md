### 1. arXiv:2606.02621 — "A Fibonacci theorem for Collatz trajectories via modular graph structure"
**Author:** Manuel-Alejandro Reyes Jiménez  
**Submitted:** May 28, 2026 (math.NT)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02621

**Summary:** For each m ≥ 1, exactly F(m+1) odd integers in {1,...,2^m} have Collatz orbits that avoid residue class 4 (mod 6) during steps 2 through m. The proof constructs the Collatz transition graph G modulo 6, identifies its unique absorbing strongly-connected component G'={1,2,4,5}, and removes vertex 4. The subgraph G'∖{4} has spectral radius φ=(1+√5)/2 vs. ρ(G')=2; this spectral gap generates the Fibonacci count. Proportion of such orbits decays at rate (φ/2)^m.

**QA Mapping Potential:**
- *Singularity removal ↔ QA Singularity blockade*: Vertex 4 is the "removed fixed point" in the mod-6 graph; after removal, Fibonacci dynamics emerge. This is structurally identical to QA's Singularity (9,9) — remove the fixed point from the orbit structure, and the remaining pairs satisfy the 24-cycle Fibonacci periodicity (π(9)=24, now formally proved in cert [128]). The Singularity blockade cert (`qa_fst_orbit_analysis` line in MEMORY) showed "decomposition forbids singularity" — same obstruction principle.
- *Modular graph orbit spectrum*: The spectral radius ratio φ/2 is a continuous observer projection over the discrete QA orbit. This is exactly Theorem NT: φ is emergent from the discrete structure, not a causal input. The discrete count F(m+1) is the primary fact; φ/2 is the asymptotic observer.
- *Fibonacci/mod arithmetic junction*: The paper unifies three QA-central concepts — Fibonacci sequence, modular arithmetic, and graph orbit structure — in one spectral result. Cert [128] (π(9)=24 exact Pisano period) and the FST orbit analysis both live at this junction.
- *Absorbing SCC ↔ Cosmos orbit*: G'={1,2,4,5} is the absorbing strongly-connected component mod 6 — the entire eventual-attractor set. QA's Cosmos orbit (72 pairs in 24-cycle) is the absorbing attractor for QA dynamics. The 4=absent fixed point ↔ QA singularity at (9,9) outside the Cosmos.
- *Caveats*: The Collatz map is 3n+1 not QA's step function `((b+e-1)%m)+1`. The result is about orbit *counts* not orbit *structure*. The spectral φ is a consequence of Fibonacci arithmetic, not independently derived.

**Value rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**New cert candidate:** `qa_collatz_fibonacci_spectral_cert_v1`  
Claim: The spectral gap between the Collatz mod-6 absorbing SCC (ρ=2) and its Fibonacci subgraph (ρ=φ) mirrors the QA Singularity blockade: removing the fixed point from a modular orbit structure reveals Fibonacci dynamics (cert [128]). Certify: (C1) Collatz mod-6 fixed point = QA Singularity in role (isolated, not in Fibonacci attractor); (C2) F(m+1) count formula ↔ QA's 72-pair Cosmos structure (both exact, not asymptotic); (C3) φ as observer projection over discrete Fibonacci orbit structure (Theorem NT compliance check).

### 2. arXiv:2510.24882 — "Modular Periodicity of Random Initialized Recurrences"
**Author:** Marc T. Pudelko  
**Submitted:** October 28, 2025; v5 April 9, 2026 (math.NT)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24882

**Summary:** Extends classical Pisano period analysis to ALL m² initial pairs in (ℤ/mℤ)². Key results: (1) Perfect mirror symmetry between Fibonacci recurrence a_{n}=a_{n-1}+a_{n-2} and its parity transform a_{n}=−a_{n-1}+a_{n-2}; (2) Fractal self-similarity in prime→prime-power extension; (3) Prime moduli classified by quadratic reciprocity; (4) Distribution P(n) of minima (governed by Lucas ratios) satisfies P(n)=P(1−n); (5) Explicit counting functions for distinct periods via cyclotomic recurrences.

**QA Mapping Potential:**
- *All m² initializations = full orbit pair space*: QA classifies ALL (b,e) pairs in (ℤ/mℤ)² — 72 Cosmos pairs + 8 Satellite + 1 Singularity = 81 pairs for m=9. This paper examines all m² initializations of the same Fibonacci matrix. The "complete orbit classification" thesis is identical.
- *Mirror symmetry*: The Fibonacci parity transform a_{n}=−a_{n-1}+a_{n-2} mirrors QA's b−e reflection symmetry in the step map. The perfect symmetry result constrains which orbit families are independent — directly analogous to QA's orbit partition theorem (cert [128] `fib_mat_pow_eq_one_iff` establishes a group isomorphism).
- *Prime→prime-power fractal*: QA uses mod 9 (=3²) and mod 24 (=8×3). The fractal self-similarity from p to p² is exactly the step from mod 3 to mod 9 in QA's modular hierarchy. Cert family candidates for mod-3 → mod-9 lifting are latent in the orbit pack.
- *Cyclotomic period counting*: Connects to QA's `qa_fibonacci_matrix_orbit_periods_cert_v1` — the distinct period counting problem is the same.
- *Scan note*: Paper missed prior scans (submitted Oct 2025, v5 Apr 2026) because the "random recurrences" framing obscured the Pisano-period content. Fixed by adding "Pisano" + "Fibonacci modular periodicity" to the keyword battery.
