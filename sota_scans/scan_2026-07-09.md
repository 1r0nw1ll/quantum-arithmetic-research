### 2. arXiv:2602.03655 — Sequential Group Composition: A Window into the Mechanics of Deep Learning
**Authors**: Giovanni Luca Marchetti et al.
**Posted**: February 2026
**Rating**: MEDIUM
**Status**: MEGA-SLIP — missed all 18 previous scans

**Summary**: Introduces sequential group composition task where networks receive a sequence of finite group elements and must predict their cumulative product. Proves: two-layer networks learn group composition **one irreducible representation at a time**; width exponential in sequence length is required for perfect generalization. Results hold for any finite group, including cyclic groups Z/mZ.

**QA Mapping Potential**: MODERATE. Confirms character-orbit decomposition is what two-layer MLPs discover for group composition — reinforces the June 2026 Convergence Triple (scan #17: three independent groups proving the same fact). The "one irrep at a time" result is the sequential-composition companion to He et al.'s per-neuron almost-sure convergence (arXiv:2606.02993). Less novel given existing coverage but closes the reference from scan #18 search fixes.

**Cert candidate**: Adds to `qa_nn_group_spectral_cert_v1` [523] evidence base. No standalone cert needed.
