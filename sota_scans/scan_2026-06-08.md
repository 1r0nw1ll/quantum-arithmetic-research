### 1. arXiv:2606.02621 — A Fibonacci theorem for Collatz trajectories via modular graph structure
**Rating: MEDIUM** (new in window; posted May 28, 2026)
**Author:** Manuel-Alejandro Reyes Jiménez (independent)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02621

**Summary:** Proves that for each m≥1, exactly F(m+1) odd integers in {1,…,2^m} have Collatz orbits that avoid residue class 4 (mod 6) during steps 2,…,m, where F(m+1) is the (m+1)-th Fibonacci number. The proportion of such integers decays at rate (φ/2)^m where φ=(1+√5)/2. The proof constructs a directed graph G of all Collatz transitions mod 6, identifies a unique absorbing strongly connected component G'=G[{1,2,4,5}], and shows that removing vertex 4 yields a subgraph of spectral radius φ (vs ρ(G')=2). The Fibonacci count follows from this spectral gap. An explicit bijection Ψ_m:{1,…,6·2^m}→𝒫_m(G) encodes integers as directed paths in G.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Genuine structural overlap at three levels:
1. **Modular directed graph → QA state graph**: The Collatz mod-6 directed graph with its absorbing SCC is structurally analogous to QA's Z/24Z and Z/8Z directed state graphs. Both encode integer dynamics as directed paths through modular state spaces. The absorbing SCC in the Collatz case = the periodic orbit in QA's case.
2. **Fibonacci orbit counting → Pisano/orbit period chain**: The Fibonacci count F(m+1) of orbits with specific properties extends QA's use of Fibonacci numbers in orbit counting (cert [291] `qa_fibonacci_matrix_orbit_periods_cert_v1`). The φ spectral radius emerges independently from modular graph analysis, corroborating `qa_golden_ring_orbit_cert_v1` (scan #5 candidate).
3. **Integer-to-directed-path bijection → QA path encoding**: The explicit bijection between integers and directed paths in G is analogous to QA's representation of states as sequences of T-operator steps.

**Why MEDIUM, not HIGH:** (1) Collatz map (3n+1) is not a cyclic group endomorphism; the mod-6 graph does not have the cyclic symmetry of Z/24Z. (2) The key result is about *excluding* a residue class, not about the full orbit structure QA certifies. (3) The φ emergence is via spectral analysis of a non-QA graph, providing corroboration but not a direct structural proof for QA's claims.

**Cert scaffolding:** No new standalone cert. Add as corroborating reference to `qa_golden_ring_orbit_cert_v1` (scan #5 candidate) and `qa_fibonacci_matrix_orbit_periods_cert_v1` [291]. The φ/2 spectral decay rate is a new external confirmation of the golden-ratio orbit connection.

### 2. arXiv:2605.01148 — Arithmetic in the Wild: Llama Uses Base-10 Addition to Reason About Cyclic Concepts
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped **scans #4–9**; posted May 1, 2026)
**Authors:** Researchers studying Llama-3.1-8B cyclic arithmetic reasoning
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01148

**Summary:** Studies how Llama-3.1-8B reasons over cyclic concepts (days of week, months, hours, musical notes). Key finding: instead of computing modular addition in the period of the cyclic concept (i.e., using the orbit-specific geometry), the model re-uses a generic base-10 addition mechanism, operating independently of concept-specific geometry. The cyclic period is effectively ignored by the model's internal representation.

**QA Mapping Potential:** This paper provides an empirical instantiation of the Observer Projection Firewall (Theorem NT). The LLM's base-10 addition mechanism is an "observer layer" that processes cyclic phenomena (days mod 7, months mod 12) without entering the discrete cyclic layer. The finding that the model fails to use period-specific geometry despite correct outputs is a concrete example of a continuous observer projection operating on a discrete input: the discrete orbit structure (Z/7Z, Z/12Z) exists, but the observer uses generic Base-10 arithmetic to approximate it, violating T2-b (float × modulus cast) implicitly. This provides empirical grounding for the `qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1` (scan #8 candidate), specifically for the claim that observer-layer computation is inherently group-agnostic while the matched group (Z/nZ) determines the correct transform.

**Why LOW-MEDIUM, not MEDIUM:** This is about LLM behavior, not about the mathematical structure of discrete dynamics. The T2/Theorem NT connection is at the conceptual level, not the formal algebraic level. The grokking chain is already saturated from scans #3–7.

**Cert scaffolding:** No standalone cert. Add as empirical example to `qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1` human tract (observer-layer failure to use matched group = T2 violation in practice).
