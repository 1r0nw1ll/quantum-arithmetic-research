### 1. arXiv:2604.01548 — "On the Dynamics of Linear Finite Dynamical Systems Over Galois Rings"
**Authors:** Jonas Kantic, Claudio Qureshi, Daniel Panario, Fabian Legl  
**Submitted:** April 1, 2026 (math.DS; cs.DS; math.AC)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01548

**Summary:** Extends the analysis of linear finite dynamical systems (LFDS) from fields and cyclic modules to Galois rings (GR(p^k,r) = Z/p^k Z when r=1). The core problem: given a linear map T: x ↦ Ax on (GR(p^k,r))^n, compute the cycle lengths and transient heights of its functional graph. The authors give explicit algorithms for both quantities, extending classical results from finite fields (where every orbit is a cycle, no transients) to the genuinely more complex Galois ring setting (where transients exist and depend on the p-adic structure). Key structure: the functional graph decomposes into ρ-trees (transient branches) rooted on cycles, with cycle length determined by the order of A modulo the maximal ideal and transient height by the p-adic valuation of eigenvalues.

**QA Mapping Potential — HIGH:**
- *QA T-operator is an LFDS over Z/9Z*: QA's step map σ: (b,e) ↦ (b+e mod 9, e) (0-indexed) is the linear map [[1,1],[0,1]] acting on (Z/9Z)². Z/9Z = GR(3²,1) is a Galois ring with p=3, k=2. This paper directly provides the algorithmic framework for computing QA's orbit structure as a Galois ring LFDS.
- *Functional graph = QA orbit classification*: The paper's "functional graph" (cycles + transient trees) maps directly to QA's 81-pair orbit partition. For the Cosmos orbit (72 pairs, period 24): these are the 24-cycles. For the Satellite (8 pairs, period 8): 8-cycles. For the Singularity (9,9): a fixed point (1-cycle). The transient height = 0 for all QA orbits (every QA pair is periodic, no transients), which is a special property of the QA T-operator over Z/9Z that this paper's framework can certify.
- *Zero transients as a certifiable QA property*: The paper shows that transients exist when eigenvalues have non-trivial p-adic valuation in the Galois ring. The QA matrix [[1,1],[0,1]] over Z/9Z has characteristic polynomial (x-1)² with repeated eigenvalue 1. Since 1 is a unit in Z/9Z, the orbit is entirely cyclic — zero transients. This certifiable fact (all 81 pairs are periodic, zero transients) is a new certifiable QA claim.
- *Cycle length formula*: The paper gives the order of [[1,1],[0,1]] as a function of the Galois ring structure. For Z/9Z: the order of this matrix must divide lcm(24,8)=24. The exact period structure (72 pairs at period 24, 8 pairs at period 8, 1 pair at period 1) follows from the paper's cycle-length algorithm.
- *Z/24Z via CRT*: Z/24Z = Z/8Z × Z/3Z. Z/8Z = Z/2³ is GR(2³,1). The paper covers all prime-power Galois rings. So mod-24 QA is covered by applying the paper to GR(2³,1) and GR(3,1) separately and combining via CRT.
- *Caveats*: The paper covers general linear maps; QA's specific Jordan form [[1,1],[0,1]] is the simplest non-trivial case (a single Jordan block). The cert would instantiate the general framework to this specific case, not add new algorithmic content.

**Value rating: HIGH**  
**New cert candidate:** `qa_linear_fd_galois_ring_cert_v1`  
Claim: QA's σ-operator on (Z/9Z)² is an LFDS over the Galois ring GR(3²,1) with zero transient height (every orbit is a cycle) and cycle lengths {24,8,1} corresponding to the Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity orbit partition. Certify: (GR_1) σ-functional graph has zero transients: all 81 pairs are purely periodic under σ; (GR_2) cycle lengths exactly {1,8,24}: 1 pair at period 1, 8 pairs at period 8, 72 pairs at period 24; (GR_3) Galois ring structure: Z/9Z = GR(3²,1) and the Jordan form [[1,1],[0,1]] implies unit eigenvalue = no p-adic transient depth; (GR_4) Z/24Z coverage via CRT: GR(2³,1) × GR(3,1) decomposition reproduces the mod-24 orbit structure. All checks pure integer iteration.

### 2. arXiv:2606.23887 — "Weyl Orbit Particles"
**Author:** Martin T. Luu (University of California, Davis)  
**Submitted:** June 2026 to arXiv (published: J. Phys. A: Math. Theor. 58 (2025): 375201)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23887  
**Categories:** math-ph; math.RT

**Summary:** Studies the mass spectrum of affine Toda field theory. The classical result: masses correspond to eigenvectors of the Cartan matrix, with particles corresponding to Coxeter element orbits in the root set. Luu generalizes this to all Weyl group elements (not just Coxeter elements), showing particles still correspond to orbits in the root set under the chosen Weyl group element. Works out explicit examples for the infinite family D_{2n}(a_{n-1}) of primitive regular conjugacy classes in Weyl groups of complex simple Lie algebras.

**QA Mapping Potential — LOW-MEDIUM:**
- *Weyl orbit ≈ QA orbit*: The paper's "particles = orbits in root set under Weyl group element" is structurally analogous to QA's three orbit classes (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity), which are orbits under the QA step map σ acting on the state set. Both are orbit classifications of a finite dynamical system under a group action.
- *D_{2n}(a_{n-1}) family*: For the case of E8 (the exceptional simple Lie algebra directly used in QA's harmonic index), the Weyl group W(E8) acts on 240 E8 roots. The orbit structure of W(E8) on the roots is adjacent to QA's alignment targets (QA 4D tuples project to 8D and compute cosine similarity with these 240 roots). The paper extends to non-Coxeter Weyl elements — potentially giving access to sub-symmetry orbit structures within E8.
- *Mass spectrum via Cartan matrix*: The mass formula is a continuous eigenvalue computation — this is an observer-projection (Theorem NT). The discrete structure is the orbit partition; the masses are emergent.
- *Caveats*: This is physics-first (affine Toda field theory). No connection to modular arithmetic, cyclic groups, or discrete-to-continuous bridges beyond the philosophical level. The E8 overlap is adjacent to existing scan #5 work (arXiv:2504.16513 E8 bracket, arXiv:2605.09333/58 Corradetti E8 cluster) but adds no new certifiable claim. The paper was published in J.Phys.A 2025 — it's not a brand-new result, just newly posted to arXiv.

**Value rating: LOW-MEDIUM**  
**No standalone cert candidate.** Adjacent to existing E8 cert cluster [496] + arXiv:2504.16513.

### 3. Directed Torus Hamilton Decomposition Series (MEGA-SLIP — 4 papers)
**Papers:**
- arXiv:2603.24708 (March 2026) — "Hamilton decompositions of the directed 3-torus: a return-map and odometer view" by SangHyun Park
- arXiv:2604.27140 (April 2026) — "Hamilton decompositions of the directed 5-torus for odd modulus"
- arXiv:2605.00660 (May 2026) — "Hamilton decompositions of the directed 7-torus at odd modulus via root-flat certificates"
- arXiv:2606.21583 (June 19, 2026) — "Short Second Proof of the Odd-Modulus Directed Torus Hamilton Decomposition Theorem"

**Summary:** A 4-paper series (all by SangHyun Park, math.CO/math.GR) proving that the directed d-dimensional torus D_d(m) — the Cayley graph of (Z/mZ)^d with the d standard generators — admits a decomposition into d arc-disjoint directed Hamilton cycles, for every d≥2 and odd m≥3. The general theorem (final paper) completes the series. Proof technique evolves from return-map + odometer (d=3, any m) to zero-set selectors and root-flat certificates (d=5, d=7, odd m). Key combinatorial tool: a fixed-row-sum selection theorem for replicated supports.

**QA Mapping Potential — LOW-MEDIUM:**
- *State space overlap*: D_d(m) is the Cayley graph of (Z/mZ)^d. For d=2, m=9: D_2(9) = Cayley graph of (Z/9Z)², which is QA's 81-pair state space. The theorem says this Cayley graph has a 2-Hamilton-cycle decomposition. But QA's dynamics are generated by a specific map σ (not the full Cayley graph), so the Hamilton decomposition result is about the underlying graph structure, not QA's orbit dynamics.
- *Proof technique*: The "return-map and odometer view" in arXiv:2603.24708 is methodologically aligned with QA's orbit analysis (projection onto a layer, return-time counting). Could inform the human-tract exposition of cert [261] orbit stratification.
- *Odd modulus*: The general theorem requires odd m. QA's mod-24 (even) is NOT covered by the odd-modulus theorem. QA's mod-9 (odd) IS covered. The odd modulus restriction may reflect a genuine obstruction for even moduli — relevant to why mod-24 has richer orbit structure.
- *Caveats*: The Hamilton decomposition result proves existence of arc-disjoint Hamilton cycles in the Cayley graph — this is about graph-theoretic properties of the state space, not about QA's specific dynamical map. No direct QA orbit classification result is certifiable from this. The series is a 4-paper mega-slip (March–June 2026) but the relevance is peripheral.

**Value rating: LOW-MEDIUM**  
**No standalone cert candidate.** Note for cert [261] orbit stratification human tract: the return-map methodology is aligned with QA's orbit analysis.  
**Root cause:** math.CO/math.GR not in systematic sweep. "Directed torus" + "Hamilton decomposition" not in search battery.  
**Fix:** Add "directed torus" + "Cayley graph" + "Hamilton decomposition modular" to arXiv search battery.
