### 2. arXiv:2606.10193 — A Modular Structure Theorem for Minimal Periodic Decompositions
**Rating: MEDIUM** (new in window; June 8, 2026)
**Authors:** C. F. Colle, E. Garibaldi
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10193

**Summary:** Proves Nivat's conjecture for the case P_η(4,n) ≤ 4n: any 2D configuration η: ℤ² → 𝒜 with rectangular pattern complexity satisfying this bound is periodic. Uses the algebraic framework of Kari-Szabados (Laurent polynomials over ℤ/mℤ) plus recent advances on one-sided nonexpansive directions. The key intermediate structural result: for any non-periodic configuration with low convex pattern complexity and integer-valued alphabet contained in ℤ₊, there exists a configuration in the orbit closure with a ℤ-minimal periodic decomposition.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Genuine structural overlap at three levels:
1. **Kari-Szabados Z/mℤ polynomial algebra**: The algebraic framework used in the proof operates on Laurent polynomials over ℤ/mℤ — exactly QA's algebraic domain. The proof technique is to analyze period structure using polynomial factorizations in ℤ/mℤ ring extensions.
2. **"Integer-valued alphabet contained in ℤ₊" forcing periodicity**: This is a direct analogue of QA's No-Zero axiom (A1: states in {1,...,N}). The paper proves that ℤ₊-valued configurations with bounded complexity MUST have periodic decomposition — providing theoretical grounding for why QA's orbit structure must be periodic.
3. **Orbit closure → ℤ-minimal periodic decomposition**: The "orbit closure" concept (configurations reachable by limit operations from a given configuration) directly corresponds to QA's state reachability under T-operator iteration. The theorem says low-complexity configurations have periodic structure in their orbit closure — grounding QA's 24-cycle/8-cycle/1-cycle classification as the minimal periodic structures of low-complexity Z/mℤ dynamics.

**Why MEDIUM, not MEDIUM-HIGH:** The paper is about 2D configurations (ℤ²→𝒜), while QA operates on 1D orbits (sequences in Z/24Z). The pattern complexity bound P_η(4,n) is for 2D rectangular patterns; QA's 1D orbit complexity is simpler and this result is over-powered for the 1D case. The connection is real but requires dimensional reduction argument.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** `qa_periodic_decomposition_complexity_cert_v1` — certifies that QA's 1D orbit sequences over Z/mℤ satisfy the Kari-Szabados complexity bounds that force periodicity; the Cosmos 24-cycle and Satellite 8-cycle are the minimal periodic decompositions of the Z/24Z and Z/8Z dynamics under the No-Zero constraint. **MEDIUM priority.** Distinct from `qa_orbit_stratification_cert_v1` [261] (which is about classification) and `qa_circulant_symplectic_orbit_cert_v1` (which is about symplectic structure).
