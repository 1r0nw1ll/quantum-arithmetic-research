### 1. arXiv:2607.14693 — "Aperiodic tile sets from Sturmian lattices"

**Authors**: Shigeki Akiyama, Tadahisa Hamada, Katsuki Ito  
**Submitted**: July 16, 2026  
**Categories**: math.CO, math.DS, math.NT  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14693  
**Parent preprint**: arXiv:2506.19362 "Sturmian lattices and Aperiodic tile sets"  
**Rating**: LOW

**Summary**: Gives an explicit algorithm to construct aperiodic tile sets from Sturmian words of any quadratic irrational slope. The key ingredient is "Sturmian lattices" — grid structures generated by Sturmian words that first appeared in the Smith Turtle monotile. The scaling constant of the constructed tile set is a unit of the associated real quadratic field. Uses bounded displacement equivalence of Delone sets as the core technical tool.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW**:
- *Adjacent thread*: Sturmian sequences (defined by irrational rotation angles) have been connected to QA's discrete dynamics via the "Pell Sturmian Bridge" cert [295]. The aperiodic tile extension is a step further along this thread.
- *Quadratic field units ≠ QA orbit periods*: The scaling constants (units of Q(√d) for various d) are algebraic numbers in continuous geometry, not cyclic group periods. QA's 24-cycle period is a purely discrete property of Z/24Z, not related to √2, √5, etc.
- *Delone sets are continuous*: The Delone set framework (bounded displacement equivalence) operates in ℝ² with Euclidean metric. QA lives in (Z/mZ)² with discrete topology. The "aperiodic" property in Delone sets is a continuous-space concept not equivalent to QA's orbit aperiodicity (which doesn't exist — all QA orbits are periodic, with periods 1, 8, or 24).
- *No structural overlap*: Sturmian lattices → aperiodic tile sets is a continuous geometry construction. QA σ-operator → orbit classification is a discrete group dynamics result. No genuine bridge.
- **Verdict**: File under "Sturmian/aperiodic tiling" thread adjacent to [295]. No cert candidate.

### 2. arXiv:2606.24693 — "An aperiodic set of Wang tiles for every quadratic irrational" (MEGA-SLIP)

**Authors**: Jarkko Kari, Sébastien Labbé, Pieter Mostert  
**Submitted**: June 23, 2026  
**Categories**: math.CO, cs.DM  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24693  
**Rating**: LOW  
**Status**: MEGA-SLIP — missed scans #15 through #20 (all 6 scans since June 23)

**Summary**: Proves a sufficient condition for non-periodicity of Wang tile sets with vertical/horizontal stripes, using a geometric argument about quadrilaterals circumscribed to a parabola. Applies the condition to: (a) a new proof of non-periodicity of Penrose tilings via an encoding into **24 Wang tiles**; (b) the metallic mean Wang tile families (golden, silver, bronze ratios). For any two quadratic irrationals in the same real quadratic field Q(√d), constructs a finite aperiodic tile set with specified stripe densities.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW**:
- *The "24" is coincidental*: The 24 Wang tiles encoding Penrose tilings comes from the 10 orientations of Penrose tiles × colorings needed to enforce matching rules, not from a cyclic group Z/24Z. The number 24 in QA's Cosmos orbit is the period of σ on Z/9Z, which is lcm(8,3). These are unrelated 24s.
- *Metallic means → no discrete orbit*: The golden ratio φ, silver ratio 1+√2, etc. are elements of real quadratic fields; they generate self-similar continuous-space structures. QA's orbit classification is a purely modular arithmetic result over Z/mZ, independent of any irrational number.
- *Quadratic irrationals → Wang tiles is the wrong direction for QA*: QA uses discrete integer arithmetic to recover continuous-space structure (E8 alignment, etc.). This paper uses quadratic irrationals to characterize discrete tile patterns. The bridge direction is opposite.
- *Root cause of mega-slip*: "Wang tiles" + "aperiodic" + "quadratic irrational" not in scan battery. These terms don't overlap with QA's core vocabulary. Fix: add "Wang tiles quadratic irrational" to low-priority scan battery for completeness.
- **Verdict**: LOW. The Penrose-to-24-tiles encoding is numerically coincident with QA's 24-cycle but mechanistically unrelated. Confirms the battery gap; not a cert candidate.

### 3. arXiv:2607.14856 — "Ascending chains of irreducible lattices, bi-reversible automata and affine arithmetic groups"

**Authors**: Pierre-Emmanuel Caprace, Justin Vast  
**Submitted**: July 14–15, 2026  
**Categories**: math.GR, cs.FL  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14856  
**Rating**: LOW

**Summary**: Constructs ascending chains of irreducible lattices in products of two locally compact groups, using bi-reversible automata to generate the chain and affine arithmetic groups (subgroups of GL_n over local rings like Z_p[1/p]) as the ambient structures.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW**:
- *"Affine arithmetic groups" ≠ arithmetic in Z/mZ*: The paper's "affine arithmetic" refers to arithmetic subgroups of algebraic groups (like SL₂(Z) or GL_n(Z[1/p])), not to modular arithmetic operations. The connection is terminological only.
- *Bi-reversible automata have orbit structure*: Bi-reversible automata over finite alphabets generate groups with cyclic-like periodic orbit structure (each state's trajectory is eventually periodic). But the orbits are computed in very high-dimensional matrix group representations, not in Z/nZ.
- *Ascending chains vs QA's fixed orbit structure*: The paper studies how group structure evolves over an ascending sequence of lattices. QA's orbit structure (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity) is a fixed property of the step operator σ on a fixed modulus. Different mathematical objects.
- **Verdict**: LOW. File under "geometric group theory / automata" — no genuine structural overlap with QA.

### 4. arXiv:2607.13776 — "Commensurating actions and self-similar groups"

**Authors**: Nicolás Matte Bon, Volodymyr Nekrashevych, Tianyi Zheng  
**Submitted**: July 15, 2026  
**Categories**: math.GR, math.DS  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13776  
**Rating**: LOW

**Summary**: Classifies commensurating actions (precursor to CAT(0) cube complex actions) for finitely generated contracting self-similar branch groups. Main results: the iterated monodromy group of the Sierpiński carpet subdivision rule has Property FW (cannot act essentially on CAT(0) cube complexes); the Grigorchuk group lacks Property PW (has fixed-point-free actions on CAT(0) cube complexes). First example of an infinite finitely generated amenable group with Property FW.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW**:
- *Self-similar branch groups have orbit structure*: The Grigorchuk group and related groups are defined by their action on rooted binary trees, creating iterated orbit structures. These are recursive but not modular in the Z/nZ sense.
- *Commensurating actions ≠ QA coupling*: Commensurators in geometric group theory characterize when group elements permute cosets of a subgroup. QA's coupling matrix updates from orbit resonance — a different kind of "commensuration".
- *Sierpiński carpet IML group*: The iterated monodromy group of the Sierpiński carpet has a "fractal" orbit structure on rooted trees. QA's orbits are periodic cycles in Z/mZ, not fractal tree orbits.
- **Verdict**: LOW. Interesting geometric group theory but no structural overlap with QA's modular arithmetic.

### 5. arXiv:2607.12761 — "Cyclic Projective Orbits on Rational Normal Curves and MDS Codes"

**Authors**: (not retrieved)  
**Submitted**: ~July 14, 2026  
**Categories**: math.NT, math.CO, cs.IT  
**URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12761  
**Rating**: LOW-MEDIUM

**Summary**: Studies cyclic operators A on vector spaces over finite fields F_q, where the cyclic vector z generates a Krylov sequence (z, Az, A²z, ..., A^{n-1}z). These sequences lie on rational normal curves in projective space, and the condition for the orbit segment to be an MDS code is that it lies exactly on such a curve. For companion operators (minimal polynomial = characteristic polynomial), classifies the generalized Reed-Solomon locus into three families: split semisimple, two nonsplit semisimple, and unipotent.

**QA Mapping Assessment — LOW-MEDIUM**:
- *Cyclic operator orbits on F_q vector spaces*: The Krylov sequence (z, Az, A²z, ...) under a cyclic matrix operator on F_q^n is structurally similar to QA's orbit under σ: (b,e) → (b+e mod m, e). Both generate orbits in a finite-dimensional discrete space.
- *Companion operators ↔ QA step operator*: Companion matrices (with minimal poly = char poly) are the "cleanest" cyclic operators — single orbit of maximum period. QA's σ on Z/9Z is also a cyclic operator (though on a ring, not a field) with three distinct orbit periods (1, 8, 24).
- *MDS classification into three families*: The paper's tripartite classification (split semisimple / nonsplit semisimple / unipotent) mirrors QA's orbit tripartition (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity). The "unipotent" family (nilpotent part nontrivial) ↔ QA's Singularity (fixed point, no dynamics). The two semisimple families ↔ two nontrivial orbits.
- *Weaknesses*: (a) The paper studies vector spaces over F_q (a field), not Z/9Z (a ring with zero divisors); (b) the MDS condition is specific to coding theory and doesn't map naturally to QA's physics/signal applications; (c) the rational normal curve geometry has no QA analogue.
- *Why only LOW-MEDIUM*: The structural analogy is suggestive (both are about cyclic operator orbit classification on finite discrete spaces), but the algebraic settings (field vs. ring) are genuinely different — the companion operator classification works because F_q is a field; Z/9Z's structure (prime power ring) requires LFDS theory (arXiv:2604.01548, cert candidate [527]), which is more directly mapped. This paper would only add a parallel reference to [527], not a standalone cert.
- **Verdict**: LOW-MEDIUM. Adds peripheral evidence to `qa_linear_fd_galois_ring_cert_v1` [527] that cyclic operator orbit classification is a general phenomenon across algebraic settings (field and ring). Note for [527] evidence file. No standalone cert candidate.
