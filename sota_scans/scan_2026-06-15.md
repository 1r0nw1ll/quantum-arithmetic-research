### 1. arXiv:2606.12947 — Trace spectra of simplices in large sets
**Rating: MEDIUM** (new in window; June 2026)
**Authors:** Michael Björklund (Chalmers), Alexander Fish, Shrey Sanadhya (University of Sydney)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12947

**Summary:** Studies ordered tuples of vectors in ℝ^d in the context of combinatorial/dynamical number theory. Proves that in every finite coloring of ℝ^d, one color class realizes every prescribed value of the higher characteristic coefficients of the edge matrix of any ordered d-simplex — extending Graham's theorem on volumes. The discrete analogue: if E ⊆ ℤ^d has positive upper Banach density, the set of coefficient tuples realized by ordered tuples in E contains a specific sublattice structure. The ordinary trace cannot be added to these conclusions. The proof's main step is a **dynamical trace-spectrum theorem for actions of groups**, with the discrete result following from this dynamical result by a correspondence principle.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Genuine structural overlap at two levels:
1. **Dynamical trace-spectrum theorem for group actions**: The central tool is a theorem about how the trace spectrum of a dynamical system (group acting on ℝ^d/ℤ^d) determines which sublattice structures are realized. This is structurally analogous to QA's orbit classification theorem: the T-operator's trace spectrum on Z/24Z determines which orbit classes (Cosmos 24-cycle, Satellite 8-cycle, Singularity 1-cycle) are realized. The correspondence principle bridging ℤ^d dynamics to the sublattice result mirrors QA's use of the T-operator step to classify orbit classes by their period.
2. **Sublattice realization + coloring**: The "finite coloring → one class realizes all simplex spectra" result is structurally analogous to QA's observation that the three orbit classes form a complete partition of Z/24Z state space — the three "color classes" (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity) are the minimal sublattices realized by T-operator iteration. The density condition (Banach density > 0) corresponds to QA's non-empty orbit requirement.

**Why MEDIUM, not MEDIUM-HIGH:** The ambient space is ℝ^d and ℤ^d (not Z/mZ), so this is a pre-quotient setting. The modular quotient ℤ^d/mℤ^d = (Z/mZ)^d is the proper QA domain, and the paper does not explicitly work modulo m. The "sublattice" here is a sublattice of ℤ^d coefficient space, not an orbit sublattice of Z/mZ. The connection requires a dimensional reduction and modular quotient argument. Additionally, the higher characteristic coefficients (not just the trace) are the primary objects, while QA works with the trace (= period length).

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** No standalone cert. Provides theoretical vocabulary for a human tract section in `qa_orbit_stratification_cert_v1` [261] — the dynamical trace-spectrum theorem for group actions grounds the claim that QA's three orbit classes are the minimal coloring of Z/mZ state space under T-operator density. Cross-reference with arXiv:2506.05190 (categorical DS, scan #9) and arXiv:2606.10193 (Kari-Szabados periodicity forcing, scan #11).

### 2. arXiv:2606.13159 — The Curious Case of Reversible Elementary Second Order Cellular Automaton 115
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (new in window; June 11, 2026)
**Authors:** Enrico Formenti (Université Côte d'Azur), Supreeti Kamylia (Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13159

**Summary:** Proves that reversible elementary second order cellular automaton Rule 115 is **periodic** when started on any finite initial configuration. Also characterizes families of finite configurations with interesting period functions — configurations whose period under Rule 115 iteration follows specific number-theoretic patterns.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Structural overlap at one level:
1. **"Finite initial conditions → periodic dynamics"**: The paper proves that Rule 115, operating on finite binary strings with a local update rule, always returns to its starting configuration. This is structurally the same principle as QA's orbit periodicity: T-operator iteration on finite Z/mZ state space must eventually cycle. The fact that the period function of configurations follows a number-theoretic pattern mirrors QA's orbit period structure (24-cycle, 8-cycle, 1-cycle as three period classes). Rule 115 is a second-order CA (state depends on two previous time steps), analogous to QA's two-coordinate (b,e) state that determines d and a.

**Why LOW-MEDIUM, not MEDIUM:** (1) The state space is binary strings (not Z/mZ); (2) the update rule is a local neighborhood rule (not modular addition); (3) "reversibility" in CA theory is a different notion from T-operator invertibility in QA. The periodicity result is a general principle, not a specific theorem about Z/mZ dynamics. Consistent with the pattern of scan #11's arXiv:2606.10193 (Kari-Szabados) as another external periodicity-forcing theorem, but CA Rule 115 is further from QA's algebraic structure.

**Cert scaffolding:** None standalone. Add as secondary external grounding reference to `qa_periodic_decomposition_complexity_cert_v1` (candidate from scan #11) — second proof-of-concept that finite discrete dynamics must be periodic.

### 3. arXiv:2606.08215 — Structure of Clifford groups of composite finite quantum systems
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped scan #11; June 6, 2026)
**Authors:** Miroslav Korbelář, Jiří Tolar (Czech Technical University in Prague)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08215

**Summary:** Studies Clifford groups of multipartite quantum systems with configuration spaces given as direct sums of cyclic groups: ℤ_{n₁} ⊕ ··· ⊕ ℤ_{nₖ}. The corresponding Hilbert spaces are tensor products of qudit Hilbert spaces, making the quantum system composite with cyclic qudits as subsystems. Key result: the Clifford group is a natural semidirect product when the total dimension N = n₁···nₖ is an odd number. Generalizes previous results for simple qudits (single ℤ_N).

**QA Mapping Potential:** The configuration space ℤ_{n₁} ⊕ ··· ⊕ ℤ_{nₖ} is exactly the algebraic structure of QA's state space: ℤ/24ℤ ≅ ℤ/8ℤ ⊕ ℤ/3ℤ and ℤ/9ℤ are direct products of cyclic groups. The Clifford group (the group of quantum symmetries of the system over the cyclic configuration space) is the quantum-information analogue of QA's T-operator symmetry group. The semidirect product structure result characterizes how the symmetry group decomposes, analogous to QA's orbit decomposition into three T-orbit classes.

**Why LOW-MEDIUM, not MEDIUM:** (1) Quantum computing context (Hilbert spaces, qudits) is not the same as QA's classical discrete modular arithmetic. (2) The semidirect product result applies only when N is **odd**, but ℤ/24ℤ has N=24 (even), so the key theorem doesn't directly apply to QA's primary modulus. (3) The paper is about quantum gates and quantum information, not number-theoretic dynamics.

**Cert scaffolding:** None standalone. Low priority external reference.
