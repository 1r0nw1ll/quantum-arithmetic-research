### 1. arXiv:2606.23044 — "Prime Fourier Embeddings: A Principled Basis for Modular Arithmetic"

**Authors:** Hyunsang Hwang, Suhyun Bae, Donghun Lee  
**Submitted:** June 2026  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23044  
**Categories:** cs.LG

**Summary:** Introduces Prime Fourier Embeddings (PFE), which encode integers as prime-indexed (cos, sin) pairs derived from harmonic analysis over prime-cyclic factor groups. The key theorem: any linear map equivariant with respect to the product group action induced by PFE must be block-diagonal with one independent block per prime — a direct consequence of Schur's lemma applied to the character decomposition. For square-free composite moduli, the Chinese Remainder Theorem predicts which prime channels are task-relevant. Empirical validation: specialization ratios exceeding 500× between task-relevant and irrelevant channels, with perfect in-distribution test accuracy across all square-free composite moduli tested. Addresses the failure of standard embeddings to expose algebraic number structure.

**QA Mapping Potential — HIGH:**
- *CRT decomposition = QA orbit structure*: Z/24Z = Z/8Z × Z/3Z (prime power factors 2³ and 3¹). PFE produces two independent channel blocks: p=2 (handling the Z/8Z factor) and p=3 (handling the Z/3Z factor). QA's mod-24 orbit structure — Cosmos period 24 = lcm(order in Z/8Z component, order in Z/3Z component) — is exactly the structure PFE encodes. The CRT decomposition that PFE uses is the same decomposition used in cert [499] and cert [504].
- *Schur's lemma → character decomposition = QA orbit partition*: The paper proves that equivariant maps block-diagonalize onto irreducible characters. QA's three orbit classes (Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity) ARE the orbit partition of Z/9Z under its irreducible character decomposition: the Cosmos = pairs whose character order is 24; Satellite = order 8; Singularity = fixed point. The paper gives a principled mathematical basis for why this partition is the natural one.
- *Principled embedding for QA state input*: If QA states (b,e) are embedded via PFE before being processed by a neural network (e.g., QA signal injection into a coupling network), the network is guaranteed by Schur's lemma to learn block-diagonal weights that respect the QA orbit partition. This is a certification of the *input representation* choice for QA-NN integration.
- *Caveats*: PFE is a representation theory result for MLPs learning modular arithmetic; the cert claim instantiates it to Z/9Z and Z/24Z specifically. The 500× specialization ratio is empirical; the block-diagonality is the proven theorem.

**Value rating: HIGH**  
**New cert candidate:** `qa_pfe_crt_character_cert_v1` [507]  
Claim: QA's Z/9Z state space under PFE block-diagonalizes into character orbits (p=3 channel) matching QA's Cosmos/Satellite/Singularity partition; QA's Z/24Z state space block-diagonalizes into CRT factor channels (p=2 and p=3) with period structure lcm(ord_8, ord_3) = 24. ### 2. arXiv:2606.02993 — "Neural Networks Provably Learn Spectral Representations for Group Composition"

**Authors:** Jianliang He, Leda Wang, Fengzhuo Zhang, Siyu Chen, Zhuoran Yang (Yale University)  
**Submitted:** June 2026  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02993  
**Categories:** cs.LG, math.GR

**Summary:** Proves that a two-layer neural network trained to predict group composition (a · b for group elements a,b) learns in the Fourier domain. The training dynamics are governed by a Riemannian gradient ascent on a representation-theoretic energy functional. Under random initialization, the training flow drives each neuron to converge **almost surely** to a **single irreducible representation**. For Abelian groups, a complete population-level description is given: random initialization promotes uniform diversification across all nontrivial irreducible representations, with phase alignment and representation competition emerging at exponential convergence rates. The paper extends prior results (which covered only Z/p for prime p) to arbitrary finite Abelian groups. Code: github.com/Y-Agent/nn-group-representation-learning.

**QA Mapping Potential — HIGH:**
- *Z/9Z and Z/24Z are Abelian groups → theorem applies directly*: The paper's complete result covers **arbitrary finite Abelian groups**. Z/9Z (QA mod-9 state group) and Z/24Z (mod-24) are both finite Abelian groups. The theorem says: NNs learning composition on these groups will, under random init, converge almost surely to neurons tuned to individual irreducible characters.
- *Irreducible characters of Z/9Z = QA orbit invariants*: The irreducible characters of Z/9Z are χ_k: m ↦ ω^{km} for k=0,...,8 (ω = primitive 9th root of unity). QA's orbit partition is the character-theoretic partition: the Singularity {(9,9)} = kernel of all nontrivial characters; Satellite pairs = character order 8; Cosmos pairs = character order 24. Neurons that converge to individual irreducibles are, in QA terms, orbit classifiers.
- *Proof of uniform diversification = proof of orbit completeness*: The paper proves neurons diversify uniformly across all nontrivial representations. In QA terms: a network that learns modular arithmetic over Z/9Z will develop neurons covering all three orbit classes (not just the most frequent Cosmos orbit). This is a certifiable consequence of representation theory.
- *Companion and upgrade to cert [501]*: cert [501] (`qa_algebraic_diversity_observer_cert_v1`) certifies the empirical observation that neural networks implicitly cover Z/24Z's full replacement theorem. This paper provides the **theoretical proof** for why this happens: uniform diversification is provably exponentially convergent under random init.
- *Caveats*: The paper covers group composition a · b; QA's step map σ: (b,e) → (b+e mod m, e) is a specific Abelian group operation (addition in Z/mZ). The theory applies because addition in Z/mZ IS the group law. The per-neuron convergence to a single irreducible is the key certified claim.

**Value rating: HIGH**  
**New cert candidate:** `qa_nn_group_spectral_cert_v1` [508]

### 3. arXiv:2606.08985 — "Beyond Neural Collapse: Task-Intrinsic Geometry Governs Neural Representations in Modular Arithmetic"

**Authors:** Hu Tan, Kuo Gai, Shihua Zhang (Chinese Academy of Sciences)  
**Submitted:** June 2026  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08985  
**Categories:** cs.LG

**Summary:** Shows that neural networks learning modular addition do NOT develop the simplex equiangular tight frame predicted by Neural Collapse theory. Instead, networks compress to a **two-dimensional cyclic geometry** in which both classifier weights and token embeddings lie on circles. Formalizes a layerwise non-uniform training mechanism: downstream classifier weights are driven by dense cross-entropy gradients into a rank-2 equiangular configuration before upstream embeddings fully reorganize; once this "classifier plane" forms, backpropagated feature gradients constrain embedding motion to the same plane while weight decay suppresses orthogonal components.

- *"Two-dimensional cyclic geometry" = QA state space geometry*: QA's state is a 2D pair (b,e) ∈ (Z/mZ)², and its orbit structure is cyclic (period 24 for Cosmos, period 8 for Satellite). The paper explicitly identifies the geometry that NNs discover as **two-dimensional and cyclic** — the exact geometry of QA's state space. This is not a keyword match; it is a structural match.
- *Rank-2 = QA's 2D coordinate pair*: The "rank-2 equiangular configuration" in the classifier plane directly corresponds to QA's derived coordinate pair structure: (b,e) as a 2D basis, with d=b+e and a=b+2e as derived coords. The rank-2 constraint is the NN's discovery of the minimal dimensionality needed to represent cyclic modular structure.
- *Circle embeddings = cyclic orbit traces*: Token embeddings on circles = modular states tracing cyclic orbits. In QA: the Cosmos orbit traces a 24-cycle on the torus (Z/9Z)²; projected to any 2D embedding it traces an ellipse/circle. The paper certifies that NNs discover this circular trace without being told.
- *Certifiable claim*: For any NN trained on modular addition with input (b,e) ∈ Z/9Z × Z/9Z and output (b+e mod 9), the embedding layer converges to a rank-2 cyclic geometry; this geometry is the 2D projection of QA's Cosmos orbit (72 pairs, period 24). - *Caveats*: The paper focuses on modular addition (not QA's joint (b,e) evolution); the "cyclic geometry" language is descriptive rather than character-theoretic (the paper doesn't use the word "character"). The cert would need to map the paper's rank-2 equiangular frame to QA's character basis.

**New cert candidate:** `qa_cyclic_geometry_nn_cert_v1` [509]  
Claim: NNs trained on modular addition over Z/9Z converge to a rank-2 cyclic embedding geometry that is the 2D projection of QA's Cosmos orbit; the "classifier plane" is spanned by the real and imaginary parts of the dominant character χ₁ of Z/9Z. ### 4. arXiv:2606.17399 — "The Discrete-Log Clock: How a Transformer Learns Modular Multiplication"

**Authors:** Huu Danh Nguyen (Stanford University)  
**Published:** ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17399  
**Categories:** cs.LG, cs.AI

**Summary:** Shows that the "dense" Fourier spectrum previously reported for transformers learning modular multiplication is an artifact of analyzing in the wrong basis. The natural Fourier transform for multiplication is the **multiplicative character transform** (not the additive DFT). Applied to a grokked transformer trained on a · b mod 113: the embedding spectrum becomes highly sparse (4 key frequencies, 96.9% of MLP neurons cleanly tuned to a single multiplicative frequency). The mechanism: the transformer learns to reduce multiplication to addition in discrete-log space — a "Discrete-Log Clock" algorithm. The log map (discrete log base g, where g is a primitive root mod 113) converts the multiplicative group into an additive one, then applies the Clock algorithm for addition.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM-HIGH:**
- *Discrete-log ↔ QA additive structure*: QA's σ-operator is purely additive: (b,e) → (b+e mod m, e). This is already in the additive domain that the Discrete-Log Clock maps TO. QA's analysis is in the "post-discrete-log" space where the Clock algorithm operates. The paper thus provides the multiplicative analogue of what QA already certifies for additive orbits.
- *Multiplicative character transform = additive character transform after log*: Z/pZ* (multiplicative group) has characters φ_k: g^n ↦ ω^{kn} — these are characters of the additive group Z/(p-1)Z in discrete-log coordinates. QA uses the additive characters of Z/9Z directly. The paper confirms that the "correct basis" for modular operations is always the character basis — the same conclusion as finds #1, #2, #3 above.
- *Sparsity in correct basis*: 4 key frequencies for mod 113. For QA mod 9: 8 nontrivial characters of Z/9Z. The sparsity principle (few characters carry all signal) implies QA's orbit partition (3 orbit classes, each associated with a distinct character order) is the minimal sufficient representation.
- *Caveats*: Modular multiplication (a·b) is different from QA's modular addition (b+e). The discrete-log trick converts multiplication to addition over Z/(p-1)Z, not Z/pZ; QA operates over Z/9Z = Z/3²Z (non-prime). The multiplicative group of Z/9Z has order φ(9)=6, not 24, so the mod-24 orbit structure comes from the additive group, not the multiplicative one.

**Value rating: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**No standalone cert candidate.** Adjacent to cert cluster [499]/[501]/[502]; note for cert [501] companion discussion.

### 5. arXiv:2606.21084 — "Persistent Homology and Equivariance in Data Analysis: A Topological Introduction"

**Authors:** (Italian TDA school, GENEO framework — consistent with P. Frosini group)  
**Submitted:** June 2026  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21084  
**Categories:** math.AT, cs.LG

**Summary:** An elementary introduction to TDA through the lens of the **observer framework**. Core structure: a **perception pair** (Φ, G) where Φ = set of bounded real-valued measurements the observer can interpret, G = group of Φ-preserving bijections (the observer's equivariance group). A **GENEO** (Group Equivariant Non-Expansive Operator) is a map between perception pairs that is equivariant with the group homomorphism and non-expansive (i.e., distances cannot increase). Persistent homology arises as a GENEO applied to the filtration functional. Bridges TDA and machine learning via the equivariance-non-expansiveness structure.

**QA Mapping Potential — MEDIUM:**
- *GENEO perception pair = QA observer layer*: Theorem NT (Observer Projection Firewall) defines QA's observer layer as the interface between continuous functions and the discrete QA system. The GENEO framework formalizes this exactly: Φ = the continuous functions the observer can compute (e.g., signal amplitudes, floating-point values); G = their symmetry group; the GENEO = the projection operator from observer space to QA discrete input space.
- *Non-expansiveness = QA firewall*: The GENEO non-expansiveness condition says distances cannot increase through the operator. In QA terms: the observer projection cannot introduce new information content beyond what is in the input signal; the discrete QA layer receives no more than the observer layer can legitimately extract.
- *Equivariance group G = the symmetries QA inputs must respect*: The group G of Φ-preserving bijections corresponds to the symmetries that the QA system's observer inputs must be covariant with (e.g., time-translation invariance in signal processing, scaling invariance in finance).
- *Caveats*: This is a pedagogical introduction, not a new theorem. The GENEO framework itself predates this paper (Frosini et al., 2013 onwards). The paper's value is in establishing the perception-pair formalism as the standard language for the observer-equivariance relationship — making it a citable formal backing for Theorem NT's design, not a new mathematical result about QA.

**Value rating: MEDIUM**
