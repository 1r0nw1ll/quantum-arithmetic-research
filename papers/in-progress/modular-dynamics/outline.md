# Modular Dynamics of Fibonacci Generators over Quadratic Rings
## Paper outline — seed draft

**Working title**: "Fibonacci Dynamics in Finite Quadratic Rings and the
Geodesic–Horocycle Structure of Pythagorean Triple Trees"

**Target venue**: Journal of Number Theory / International Journal of Number Theory

---

## Core thesis

The BEDA coordinate system $(b,e)$ for Pythagorean triples simultaneously
exposes two orthogonal dynamical structures on the modular surface
$\mathbb{H}/\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$:

1. **Geodesic flow** — generated by $T = \bigl[\begin{smallmatrix}1&1\\1&2\end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$
   (QA step, $F^2$, hyperbolic, fixed points $\pm1/\varphi$). Orbits mod $m$
   = five Pythagorean families (when $m=9$).

2. **Horocycle flow** — generated by parabolic Berggren operators
   $B=\bigl[\begin{smallmatrix}1&0\\1&1\end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$,
   $C=\bigl[\begin{smallmatrix}1&2\\0&1\end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$. Orbits =
   Barning–Berggren triple tree branches.

The paper proves the orbit classification for all moduli via the
split/inert/ramified trichotomy of $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]$, and identifies
the equidistribution consequences.

### §3 The three prime cases
For the Fibonacci matrix $F$ acting on $(\mathbb{Z}/p^k\mathbb{Z})^2$:

**Case 1: Inert primes** ($p \equiv \pm 2 \pmod 5$, e.g. $p=3$)
- $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]/p\mathbb{Z}[\varphi] \cong \mathrm{GF}(p^2)$
- $F$ acts as multiplication by $\varphi$ in $\mathrm{GF}(p^2)$
- Order of $F$ in $\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})$: divides $p^2-1$
- Orbit structure: determined by multiplicative order of $\varphi$ in $\mathrm{GF}(p^2)^*$
- **$p=3$**: $\mathrm{GF}(9)$, order=24, five orbits (proved in companion paper)
- **$p=7$**: $\mathrm{GF}(49)$, order=?, predict clean orbit structure
- **$p=13$**: $\mathrm{GF}(169)$, order=?

**Case 2: Split primes** ($p \equiv \pm 1 \pmod 5$, e.g. $p=11, 19, 29, 31$)
- $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]/p\mathbb{Z}[\varphi] \cong \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$
- $F$ acts diagonally with distinct eigenvalues in $\mathbb{F}_p$
- Orbit structure: product of two cyclic groups
- More orbits, smaller sizes

**Case 3: Ramified** ($p=5$)
- $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]/5\mathbb{Z}[\varphi] \cong \mathbb{Z}[x]/(x^2-x-1, 5) \cong \mathbb{F}_5[x]/(x-3)^2$
- Local ring, not a field; $F$ acts unipotently
- Single large orbit + degenerate orbits

### §4 Orbit classification theorem
**Theorem**: Let $p$ be prime, $F=\bigl[\begin{smallmatrix}0&1\\1&1\end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$.
The orbit structure of $\langle F \rangle$ on $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^2$ is:

| $p \pmod 5$ | $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]/p$ | orbit sizes |
|---|---|---|
| $\pm 2$ (inert) | $\mathrm{GF}(p^2)$ | determined by $\mathrm{ord}_{p^2-1}(\varphi)$ |
| $\pm 1$ (split) | $\mathbb{F}_p \times \mathbb{F}_p$ | product structure |
| $0$ (ramified) | local ring | unipotent structure |

For $p^k$ with $k>1$: Hensel's lemma lifts the $p$-case.

### §5 Geometric interpretation
- $T=F^2$: hyperbolic element, geodesic fixed points $\pm 1/\varphi$
- $B,C$: parabolic Berggren generators, horocycle fixed points at cusps
- Orthogonality: $\{$geodesic orbits$\} \perp \{$horocycle orbits$\}$ as foliations
- Ratner's theorem: horocycle equidistributes ⟹ triples uniform across families

### §6 The inradius fibration
- $r = be$: inradius coordinate (cite companion paper)
- Geodesic flow preserves $\{N(b,e)\pmod{p}\}$ (norm class)
- Horocycle flow changes $r$ by parabolic shear
- Inradius fibers = "transversals" to the geodesic foliation

### §7 Equidistribution
- Consequence of Ratner: for large $X$, primitive triples $(C,F,G)$ with $G \le X$
  equidistribute across the five families (each cosmos family gets $\sim X/3 \cdot (72/81)$)
- Numerical verification table

- Tabulate: $p$, case, $|\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]/p|$, orbit sizes, $\mathrm{ord}(F)$

Note: mod-9 is p=3 with k=2 (Hensel lift of mod-3 table); the five families
at mod-9 arise from the lift, not directly from the mod-3 table (one orbit of
size 8 at mod-3 lifts to three orbits of size 24 at mod-9 plus one of size 8).

**Inert prime pattern** (p ≡ ±2 mod 5):
At mod-p: (p²-1) non-zero elements form (p²-1)/orbit_size equal cosmos orbits,
plus one fixed point (0,0). Orbit size = ord(φ) in GF(p²)*.
- p=3: orbit_size=8=(p²-1)/1, 1 cosmos orbit
- p=7: orbit_size=16=(p²-1)/3, 3 cosmos orbits
- p=13: orbit_size=28=(p²-1)/6, 6 cosmos orbits
Number of cosmos orbits: (p²-1)/orbit_size = (p-1)/gcd(p-1, ord(φ)) ?

**Split prime pattern** (p ≡ ±1 mod 5):
φ has two eigenvalues in F_p; action is diagonal; more orbits of smaller size.
The Pythagorean family structure at split primes is finer-grained.

**Ramified (p=5)**:
F acts unipotently; single large orbit (size 20=p²-p) + smaller orbits.
