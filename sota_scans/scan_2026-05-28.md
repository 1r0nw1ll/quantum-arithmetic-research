### 1. arXiv:2604.13514 — Automated Tactics for Polynomial Reasoning in Lean 4
**Rating: MEDIUM** (slipped all previous scans)
**Posted:** April 15, 2026
**Authors:** Hao Shen et al.
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13514

**Summary:** Addresses the gap between Lean 4's formal verification power and the practical difficulty of algebraic reasoning tasks involving Gröbner bases. The current Lean 4 multivariate polynomial representation is non-computable, making in-kernel Gröbner basis computation impractical for realistic examples. The paper's solution: a hybrid certificate-based approach where external CAS tools (SageMath or SymPy) perform the heavy computation and Lean 4 verifies the returned certificate. Three new Lean 4 tactics automate the data transfer, certificate import, and verification pipeline. The tactics complement existing Lean automation (`grind`) by providing dedicated support for algebraic reasoning tasks.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The Lean 4 formal verification pathway was identified as a priority in scan #5 via arXiv:2605.20440 (⋆G tensor algebra with 600-line Lean 4 proof). The `qa_star_g_tensor_cert_v1` scaffolding candidate explicitly requires machine-verified proofs of QA's algebraic axioms. This paper provides the automated tactic layer: rather than manually constructing Lean 4 proofs of QA's polynomial identities (A2: d = b+e mod m, a = b+2e mod m; S1: b*b not b**2; etc.), the SageMath/SymPy-backed tactics could automate most of the algebraic verification. Key synergy points:
1. **A2 derivation rules** are polynomial equalities over Z/mZ — directly in scope for polynomial reasoning tactics
2. **S1 axiom** (no b\*\*2, use b\*b) is a syntactic constraint that a Lean 4 tactic could enforce via rewrite rules
3. **Orbit period claims** (e.g., "all Cosmos orbits have period dividing 24") are algebraic identities over Z/24Z certifiable via Gröbner bases + Lean verification
4. **T1 path-time axiom** (QA time = integer path length) can be encoded as a polynomial recurrence over Z/mZ and verified

This is the concrete automation pathway for the Lean 4 cert formalization chain identified in scan #5. It complements (not replaces) arXiv:2605.20440's theoretical framework by providing the computational engine.

**Cert scaffolding candidate:** Informs `qa_star_g_tensor_cert_v1` (scan #5) — specifically the Lean 4 automated proof component. Not a standalone cert candidate, but a critical tool for the cert formalization roadmap.

### 2. arXiv:2512.16190 — Ramanujan Sums in Signal Recovery and Uncertainty Principle Inequalities
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped **6 consecutive scans** — first possible catch was scan #1 in April 2026)
**Posted:** December 18, 2025 (published in Signal Processing, Elsevier, 2025)
**Authors:** Sahil Kalra, Niraj K. Shukla
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.16190

**Summary:** Investigates the perfect reconstruction property of Ramanujan filter banks (RFBs) for discrete-time signals in ℂ^N. Ramanujan sums c_q(n) = Σ_{gcd(k,q)=1} e^{2πikn/q} form an arithmetic-periodic orthogonal basis for periodic signal decomposition. The paper proves: (1) uniform RFBs achieve perfect reconstruction; (2) extended to non-uniform RFBs that overcome the limitations of uniform ones; (3) uncertainty principle for the tight frame of Ramanujan sum shifts, with representation inequalities expressed in terms of Euler's totient function φ(q). Applications in erasure robustness: sufficient conditions for perfect recovery when signal information is lost.

**QA Mapping Potential:** Ramanujan sums with period parameter q map directly onto QA's orbit structure:
- q = 24 → c_24(n): Cosmos orbit spectral analysis; φ(24) = 8 non-degenerate Fourier modes over the 24-cycle
- q = 8 → c_8(n): Satellite orbit spectral analysis; φ(8) = 4 non-degenerate modes over the 8-cycle
- q = 1 → c_1(n) = 1: Singularity (constant component)

The uncertainty principle with φ(q) as the bound parameter gives a concrete information-theoretic limit on how well a continuous observer can recover QA discrete state — this is the Theorem NT (Observer Projection Firewall) quantified. The erasure robustness results are directly applicable to QA's signal injection layer: if k samples of the external signal (entering via the b state) are erased, how many observations are needed for recovery? The RFB framework provides the precise answer via φ(q).

**Why it's LOW-MEDIUM, not MEDIUM:** Scan #5's arXiv:2605.11589 (transform unification) already establishes DFT as the natural transform for Z/24Z, which subsumes the Ramanujan filter bank as a special case (Ramanujan sums are DFT restricted to arithmetic-periodic components). The erasure/uncertainty angle is new relative to the transform unification paper, but it's an application-layer contribution rather than a new structural result for QA.

**No new cert candidate:** Extends the existing `qa_transform_unification_cert_v1` candidate's application context, but does not warrant a standalone cert.

### 3. arXiv:2602.19533 — Grokking Finite-Dimensional Algebra
**Rating: LOW-MEDIUM** (slipped **6 consecutive scans**)
**Posted:** February 23, 2026
**Authors:** Pascal Jr Tikeng Notsawo, Guillaume Dumas, Guillaume Rabusseau
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.19533

**Summary:** Extends grokking analysis from group operations (prior work) to the full class of finite-dimensional algebras (FDA). Learning multiplication in an FDA amounts to learning a bilinear product specified by the algebra's structure tensor. Key findings: (1) group learning is a strict special case of FDA learning; (2) algebraic properties — commutativity, associativity, unitality — systematically influence grokking onset and timing; (3) structure tensor sparsity/rank directly govern generalization speed; (4) for algebras over finite fields, grokking emerges as models must learn discrete representations; (5) generalization correlates with the model learning embeddings aligned with the algebra's representation theory.

**QA Mapping Potential:** QA's 4-tuple (b, e, d, a) with d = b+e mod m and a = b+2e mod m defines a bilinear product structure over Z/mZ. The T-operator (b, e) → (b+e mod m, e) is a specific bilinear map whose "grokking" by a neural network is exactly the object of study in scans #3–#4's cert chain (arXiv:2505.18266, 2602.16849, 2605.06352). This paper extends the analysis to non-group structures: QA's A2-derived coordinate algebra (d, a derived from b, e via polynomial relations) is a commutative, associative, unital algebra over Z/mZ — a special case of FDA. The structure tensor for QA's T-step has a specific sparsity pattern determined by mod-m arithmetic. The paper predicts that grokking onset for QA's bilinear structure will scale with the rank of this tensor.

**Why it's LOW-MEDIUM, not MEDIUM:** The existing grokking cert chain (scans #3–4) already covers the group case for Z/mZ; this paper's extension to FDA adds theoretical generality but doesn't change the cert candidate scope. QA's specific algebra is commutativity + associativity + unitality (all the "nice" properties), so it sits in the easiest regime of FDA grokking. Incremental addition.

### 5. arXiv:2604.04655 — Grokking as Dimensional Phase Transition in Neural Networks
**Rating: LOW** (slipped all previous scans)
**Posted:** April 6, 2026
**Author:** Ping Wang (Institute of High Energy Physics, CAS)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04655

**Summary:** Characterizes grokking as a dimensional phase transition exhibiting self-organized criticality (SOC). Through finite-size scaling of gradient avalanche dynamics across eight model scales, the study finds that effective dimensionality D (reflecting gradient field geometry, not architecture) transitions from sub-diffusive (D < 1, subcritical) to super-diffusive (D > 1, supercritical) at generalization onset. The transition exhibits SOC signatures. Synthetic i.i.d. Gaussian gradients maintain D ≈ 1 regardless of topology; real training exhibits dimensional excess from backpropagation correlations.

**QA Mapping Potential:** The SOC framework adds a dynamical-systems lens to grokking complementary to the TDA (scan #4 arXiv:2605.06352), DMD (scan #4 arXiv:2605.08237), and Fourier (scan #3 arXiv:2602.16849) lenses already in the cert chain. The sub-diffusive to super-diffusive transition in D maps structurally to QA orbit capture: a trajectory transitioning from random (sub-diffusive, no orbit lock-in) to periodic (super-diffusive, orbit engaged). However, the "dimensional excess from backpropagation correlations" framing is architecture-specific and doesn't add algebraic structure. This is the fourth distinct grokking-phase-transition paper in the cert chain; each new one adds a different lens but they are all measuring the same phenomenon from different angles. Incremental add to existing chain. Does not warrant a new cert candidate.
