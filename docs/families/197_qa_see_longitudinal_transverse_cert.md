### Analogy for Theorem NT (not physical proof — see Verification Note)

In linear elasticity, longitudinal and transverse modes are orthogonal eigenmodes of the wave equation with different propagation speeds (P-waves vs S-waves). A pure compression wave cannot generate shear — structural impossibility, not convention. This part is real, mainstream physics.

Theorem NT is the QA expression of this mode orthogonality **as an analogy**: the same tuple space supports both discrete generators and continuous observers, but they are structurally separated — mixing them would be like turning a P-wave into an S-wave. Theorem NT's actual proof rests on QA's own axioms, not on See's specific aether theory (see Verification Note).

## Verification Note (2026-07-06)

**Both primary sources confirmed real**: "Electrodynamic Wave-Theory of
Physical Forces" (1917) is held by the Smithsonian, Internet Archive,
and multiple university libraries; the Astronomische Nachrichten
memoirs (1920-1926) are likewise real and confirmed via library
catalogs.

**Found and fixed a real overclaim, following the same pattern as the
[497] Steinmetz-Whittaker/Dollard investigation earlier this cycle**:
tracing the specific claim rather than just confirming the sources are
real. This cert's specific aether wave-duality theory (gravity=
longitudinal, light=transverse waves in a physical aether medium) is
**not** the same body of work as [196]'s Capture Theory. Historians of
science (MacTutor, *Journal for the History of Astronomy* — Sherrill
1999, "A Career of Controversy: The Anomaly of T.J.J. See") document
that See's aether/wave theory belongs to his later, increasingly
eccentric period that led to his professional marginalization
(dismissed from observatory positions, harsh attacks on Einstein's
relativity) — distinctly more rejected than his earlier Capture Theory,
which at least drew serious contemporary engagement (Poincaré's 1912
critique in *The Monist*). The underlying *mathematics* of
longitudinal/transverse wave orthogonality is real, mainstream
elasticity/seismology (P-waves/S-waves) — it's specifically See's
application of that mathematics to claim gravity and light are literally
two aether modes that is the rejected part.

The cert's "theorem_nt_link" section had claimed this "elevates Theorem

rests on QA's own axioms; See's wave-duality is at most a pedagogical
analogy (by way of the real P-wave/S-wave orthogonality, not the
